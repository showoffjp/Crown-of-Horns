How Crown of Horns does area art the Infinity-Engine way — a painted backdrop, a walkability search map, and a light map, with characters composited on top.
Honest note. The image below is a procedural greybox — drawn in code (Pillow) by tools/make-painted-maps.py. It nails the framing, materials, architecture and lighting target (the IE cut-away: plank floors, cut-stone walls with depth, hearth- and sconce-light) so a painter knows exactly what to make — but it is not the final art, and was never meant to be. The real, hand-painted backdrops are produced with a dedicated image tool (e.g. Grok Imagine) and dropped straight in; the engine is built to consume them. See the pipeline spec for the camera angle, resolution, and search-/light-map format.
