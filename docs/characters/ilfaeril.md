# 🛡️ Ilfaeril — *"The Verdict"*

> **Companion 5 of 7.** The elf who voted for the Wall. Living proof of the game's most dangerous truth —
> *the Wall is a **decision**, not physics.* His arc is the game's answer **"penance"** tested to
> destruction: *atonement is not the same as self-erasure.*

---

## Identity & build
- **Full name:** Ilfaeril Auvryndar-**no-longer** (he renounced his house after the vote).
- **Class:** Paladin (Oath of Redemption) / Ranger; sword-and-board with radiant smites and a longbow;
  **ten thousand years of guilt as a stat block.**
- **Joins:** **Act III** (Crown Wars, ~−10,000 DR). **Romance:** ✗ — a deliberate, aching **platonic**
  bond.

## Backstory
Ilfaeril was a lord of the elven high courts during the **Crown Wars** — the millennia of fratricidal
conflict that birthed the drow and broke the elven peoples forever. In a council of vengeance after an
atrocity, the lords voted on the fate of an enemy people — *not their lives, but their **souls.*** They
voted that the enemy's godless dead be **unmade**, denied even the dignity of a resting place, so not even
their memory would have anywhere to rest. **Ilfaeril raised his hand.** The verdict, repeated across other
courts and other wars, **hardened over millennia into the cosmic Law of the Faithless.** He is, in the most
literal sense, one of the people who *wrote* the central atrocity of the setting — by voting yes one
terrible afternoon and never voting it down.

He has spent the **ten thousand years** since as a half-ghost at the edge of the Fugue Plane, guiding lost
souls past the Wall, trying to atone for a thing too large to atone for. He does not believe atonement is
possible. He keeps trying anyway — which is either the noblest or the most self-destructive thing in the
game.

## The wound
*A single raised hand, ten thousand years of consequence.* Ilfaeril needs to learn that atonement is **not
the same as self-erasure** — that a person who has done an unforgivable thing is still *allowed to stop
paying*, allowed to rest, allowed to be **more than his worst vote.**

## The secret
He is **living proof** of the game's most dangerous idea — that the Wall is a *decision.* **[The
Unmade](the-unmade.md) reveres him as its first witness.** He fears that makes him its **instrument** — and
he's right to.

## Personal quest — *"The Verdict"* (three chapters)
- **Ch. 1 — The Witness (Act III):** Ilfaeril is the living proof the Wall is a decision; the Unmade reveres
  him; he fears what that makes him.
- **Ch. 2 — The Descendants (Act IV):** you find the souls of the people he damned — **still in the Wall,
  still dissolving, ten thousand years later**, because his vote never expired. They know his name. They
  have had a very long time to decide what they'd say to him.
- **Ch. 3 — Forgiveness or the Open Door (Act V):** the climax. The damned can **forgive** him (and he must
  *accept* it, which is harder for him than any punishment) — or he can refuse forgiveness and instead
  **hold the Wall open**, physically, at the cost of his own existence, so the souls he condemned can flee
  through to whatever lies beyond. The first is the **Oath of Redemption fulfilled;** the second is
  martyrdom, and he'll choose it gladly **unless you convince him his life is worth more than his guilt.**

## The bond (not a romance)
Ilfaeril is the game's argument that *not every deep love is romantic.* He becomes, if you let him, a kind
of **grandfather to the whole party** — ten thousand years of patience, the one who sits up with the others
on the bad nights. The heart of it is his relationship with **[Maerin](maerin.md):** the ancient man who
helped build the Wall, and the girl it swallowed, learning to forgive each other on behalf of everyone who
can't. If both survive, their epilogue is **shared.**

## Approval & fates
- **Gains:** mercy, oaths kept, humility, accepting forgiveness, protecting the condemned. **Loses:**
  cruelty, pride, despair-as-indulgence, vengeance.
- **Fates:** **forgiven and finally at rest**, sword laid down after ten thousand years (best); **martyred
  holding the Wall open**, the last one through — or not; or **lost to despair**, walking into the Wall
  himself to join the people he damned (the game treats this as a tragedy, not a triumph).

## Key relationships
- **[The Unmade](the-unmade.md)** — its first witness; reveres him; he fears being its tool.
- **[Maerin](maerin.md)** — the wall-builder and the wall-swallowed, learning to forgive each other.
- **Coronal Aedyn** — the high judge who *invents* the Wall as "merciful"; Ilfaeril's worst afternoon, live.
- **The Exile Who Forgave** — the elf who refused to vote and was cast out; Ilfaeril's road not taken.
- **[Varra](varra.md)** — the defiance to his penance; the party's sharpest, fondest needling.

> His answer to *what is a soul worth that no god claimed?* is **penance** — and his arc is the discovery
> that a soul that did an unforgivable thing is still *allowed to stop paying*, and that the most dangerous,
> most hopeful truth he carries is the proof that **the Wall can be unmade, because someone made it.**
