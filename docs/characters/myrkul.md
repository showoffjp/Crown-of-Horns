# 👁️ Myrkul — *Mask II: the Crown-Voice / the Pale Companion*

> The dead god in the relic; a secret "companion" who rides your pack from Act II; the mid-game
> true-power reveal. The easiest villain to listen to — which is exactly the point.

---

## Role
- The dead god whose residual divinity *is* the **Crown of Horns.**
- From Act II, the **Pale Companion** — a reasonable, dry, almost-kindly voice in your pack offering
  counsel that is always *technically* true and always bends the road a little toward the summit.
- Unmasked above Waterdeep in Act IV, where he drops the geniality and shows the throne he wants.

## Backstory — *the long patience*
Once there were three mortals who decided godhood was a thing that could be *taken*, and they were
right: **Bane, Bhaal, and Myrkul** — the **Dead Three** — clawed onto thrones older powers had grown
bored enough to leave unguarded. Myrkul took **death.** He was never the strongest of the three nor the
loudest-cruel; his gift was **patience.** Death does not chase anyone. Death only waits. He ruled the
dead like a miser rules a vault — counting, hoarding, in no hurry, certain that everything that lives
will eventually be added to the ledger.

He **died** in the **Time of Troubles** (1358 DR), the year the gods walked Faerûn as mortals and could
be killed — above Waterdeep — and his skull was forged into the **Crown of Horns**, into which seeped the
last sediment of his stolen divinity. And then Myrkul did the only thing he has ever been good at. He
**waited.**

## The trick he learned in the dark
A century in a relic teaches patience even to the patient. Myrkul learned the one trick that can return a
dead god to a throne he was thrown from: **not force — substitution.** A vehicle. A mortal who wants the
throne so badly, for reasons so sympathetic, that the whole world will cheer them up the steps — and who
can then be **shed**, like a snake sheds skin, at the summit.

**[Aldric Morn](aldric-morn.md)** is the best vehicle he has found in a hundred years of looking: a
grieving saint the whole world will *root for.* Myrkul intends to let Aldric ignite the Crown, walk it
into the City of Judgment, and then **shed him** and take the throne himself. Aldric does not know.

## The secret (and the joke)
Myrkul fears exactly one thing, and it is **not** you and not Kelemvor: it is **[the Unmade](the-unmade.md)** —
because the Unmade is made of the souls *his* Wall dissolved, and if it ever unwrites the law, Myrkul's
entire claim to the dead unravels with it. So for most of the game the patient old miser is, hilariously
and horribly, **on your side** against the Unmade — whispering useful truths, saving your life — purely so
that he can **betray you the instant that danger passes** and take the throne you cleared for him.

## Want vs. need
- **Want:** the throne of the dead, regained by substitution.
- (Myrkul does not *change* — he is the reminder that **not every ally is a friend, and not every enemy of
  your enemy is anything but a worse enemy, waiting.**)

## How he's played
The easiest villain to listen to. Heeding the Pale Companion grants **power and lies in equal measure** —
a Planescape-style philosopher who is also a final enemy. Every counsel is true; every truth bends the
road toward the summit he intends to take.

## Key relationships
- **[Aldric Morn](aldric-morn.md)** — his unwitting vehicle.
- **[The Unmade](the-unmade.md)** — the one thing he fears; his temporary, treacherous alliance with *you* exists only to stop it.
- **[Varra](varra.md)** — her patron may be Myrkul's own agent; her pact may be a **leash** he can yank at the worst moment.

> *"Not every ally is a friend."* Myrkul is the proof — the miser of souls who will save your life nine
> times to be present, and useful, and trusted, on the tenth, when the larger danger has passed and the
> throne stands clear.
