# 👁️ Aldric Morn — *Mask I: the Doomguide Who Said No*

> The Act I villain; a fallen paladin/cleric of Kelemvor. The charismatic, sympathetic mentor-as-monster.
> **The most terrible thing about him is not that he is wrong. About the Wall, he is *right* — and you
> may have to kill him anyway, and he will forgive you for it.**

---

## Role
- The face of the antagonist for the first act; introduced over **tea.** A grieving saint the world will *root for.*
- The unwitting vehicle for [Myrkul](myrkul.md) and for the loop you are.

## Backstory — *forty years of graves*
He was born in a **plague-cart** — literally, jolting between two dying villages, his mother burning
with the fever that would take her before he could walk. His father was a corpse-carter; before Aldric
could read, he could tell how long a body had lain by the way the air moved over it; before he was ten,
he had buried more people than most priests bless in a lifetime.

He took the grey of the **Doomguides** — Kelemvor's militant clergy, who lay the restless dead, hunt the
undead, sit with the dying, and believe that **death, properly kept, is the last and greatest mercy.**
For forty years he was the best of them on the Sword Coast: he buried plague-villages with his own
hands, comforted the dying, and never once doubted.

He married late and perfectly: **Saerin**, a cartographer who believed in latitude and weather and
*nothing else.* He found her faithlessness restful — a room in his life where no god was watching. Their
daughter, **Maerin**, took after her mother: godless, joyful, in love with a world she thought no one was
running. Aldric used to look at her laughing and think: *surely a god of mercy would not punish that.
Surely the joy is its own prayer.*

## The wound that made the villain
The winter the **Howling Pox** came back, it took Saerin in nine days and Maerin in eleven. Aldric gave
them their rites with his own hands — the only time in forty years his hands shook — and then did the
thing no Doomguide is permitted: he used the forbidden rite of **following**, to walk a little way into
the Fugue behind the newly dead and see them judged kindly.

He watched **Kelemvor** sort Saerin — who had loved him, who had been good, who had simply never knelt —
into the **Wall of the Faithless**, packed living and aware into the mortar of the damned. Then they
brought **Maerin.** Sixteen. Whose only crime was finding the world beautiful without permission.

He fell on his knees and begged. And Kelemvor — *not cruel; the god wept* — told him the truth: **the
Wall is not my law. It is older than my throne. No god made it. No god can unmake it. I am as bound by it
as your daughter is.**

A cruder man would have raged. **Aldric did arithmetic.** If the gods would not tear down the Wall, then
he would *become* what they were and tear it down himself. He found the others — the doubting Doomguides,
the families of the Faithless — gave them a name (the **Choir of the Unwalled**) and a hope and a price
they were already willing to pay, and found the lure that lets a mortal climb to godhood: the **Crown of
Horns.**

## The secret
He does **not** know the Crown is Myrkul, riding him. He does **not** know that the "vision" that sent
him into the Fugue was *seeded* — by the Unmade, by the Last Returned, by **you**, in a prior cycle. He
is a good man, weaponized by his own grief, by a parasite god, and by the loop you are. *He thinks the
arithmetic is his.*

## Want vs. need
- **Want:** tear down the Wall and save the Faithless — including his family.
- **Need:** to understand that the cure he's building is a tyranny worse than the disease — and that
  **Maerin, pulled out of the Wall, does not want to be his reason.** The most devastating scene in the
  game is the one where you can let his own resurrected daughter tell him so.

## Possible fates
- **Killed** (and he forgives you for it).
- **Redeemed** — talked down, if **Maerin** lives and you've earned it (her words can *break* him).
- **Ascended** — you *help* him win, and watch Myrkul shed him like a husk the instant he does.
- **Spared** — left to live with what he's done.

## Key relationships
- **[Maerin](maerin.md)** — his dead daughter and his whole reason; pulling her out, and letting her refuse the symbol, is the most direct line to his heart.
- **[Myrkul](myrkul.md)** — the god wearing him, unbeknownst.
- **[Sister Vayle](sister-vayle.md)** — his hunter; the keeper of the Wall he means to break.
- **[Sister Garrow](sister-garrow.md)** — his student, who *didn't* follow him and has hated herself for the obedience ever since.

> He brings you tea, and tells you he has counted every life his crusade will cost, and he will answer
> for each one, gladly — because against an eternity of children dissolving in the dark, a few thousand
> deaths is the smallest price ever asked for the largest mercy. Under all of it, he is still the man
> who sits with the dying when no one else can bear to.
