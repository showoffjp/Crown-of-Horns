# 🛡️ Sister Garrow — *"The Office of the Dead"*

> **Companion 1 of 7.** The Doomguide who *stayed* — and has hated herself for the cowardice of obedience
> ever since. Her arc is the game's answer **"duty"** tested to destruction: *faith without doubt is just
> obedience.*

---

## Identity & build
- **Full name:** Edda Garrow, Doomguide of Kelemvor.
- **Class:** Cleric (Grave domain), heavy armor, mace & shield; healing, *spare the dying*, and a unique
  reaction — **lay the Unbound** (ends an undead's turn).
- **Joins:** Prologue / Act I. **Romance:** ✅ (slow, grave, tender).

## Backstory
Edda grew up the daughter of a Sword Coast **gravedigger** — the one the plague-villages sent for when the
priests would not come. She learned death as a *craft* before she learned it as a faith: how a body
settles, how grief curdles if a corpse is left wrong, how to dig in frozen ground. She took the Doomguide
grey because it was the only church that talked about death the way her father's spade did — plainly,
without flinching. She trained under **[Aldric Morn](aldric-morn.md)**, already a legend, and loved him the
way you love a lighthouse: from a distance, gratefully, certain it would always be there.

When Aldric fell — walked into the Fugue after his dead family and came back wrong — the church gave a
quiet order, and most of his students obeyed it: *forget him.* **Garrow obeyed.** She did not follow him
into heresy; she also did not stop him, warn the families he'd recruit, or do *anything* but keep digging.
She tells herself her restraint was faith. She suspects it was **cowardice wearing faith's grey.**

## The wound
*Obedience that felt like betrayal.* Garrow has spent her life being **correct** and is no longer sure
*correct* and *good* are the same word. She needs certainty the way a drowning person needs air — which is
exactly why she distrusts it.

## The secret
She privately **agrees the Wall is monstrous.** She stays Kelemvor's servant because she believes Aldric's
cure damns *more* souls than the Wall ever has — and she is terrified that belief is just **fear wearing
the mask of faith.**

## Recruitment
You find her at the city's edge, alone, laying an Unbound child to rest with rites she's not sure she
still believes — because no one else will. She doesn't ask to join you. She asks *you* a question:
> *"When you came back out of the Wall, did it feel like mercy, or like theft?"*

Your answer determines whether she sees you as a **sign** or a **symptom.** Either way she comes — because
you are the one person alive who has *been there* and can tell her if the thing she serves is as monstrous
as she fears.

## Personal quest — *"The Office of the Dead"* (three chapters)
- **Ch. 1 — The Summons (Act II):** her own mentor — **Brother Halloran**, the gravedigger-priest who
  taught them both — has risen **Unbound** near the Fields of the Dead, torn loose by a Choir harvest.
- **Ch. 2 — The Long Walk (Act III):** tracking Halloran becomes a tour of every grave Garrow ever dug.
  You learn the **church knew** Halloran would be harvested and let it happen, to bait Aldric — *her
  faith's institution used her teacher as a worm on a hook.*
- **Ch. 3 — The Rite or the Mercy (Act IV climax):** Halloran is lucid enough to **beg** — not for rest,
  but to be allowed to keep walking, because he is **Faithless too**, and rest means the Wall. Garrow must
  choose: perform the Doomguide rite (sends him *to the Wall*, "correct" and damning); commit heresy and
  let him wander (kind and forbidden); or — if you've unlocked **[Jergal](jergal.md)**'s road — **judge him
  herself**, the first soul she ever weighs by her own conscience instead of her church's law.
  - **Outcomes:** Rite → her faith hardens into something brittle and sorrowful. Mercy → she breaks with
    the church. **Judgment → she becomes the prototype of the rewritten Law**, and weeps with a relief she's
    never felt.

## Romance arc
A slow, grave, adult romance built on *what is sacred.* It begins not with attraction but with **trust** —
the first doubt she says aloud. The midpoint, on a battlefield of the dead:
> *"I have buried a thousand people and never once let myself believe I'd see any of them again. You're
> the first thing in my life that made me want to be wrong."*

**If you pursue the Break the Loop ending, Garrow is the one who learns your true name and gives you the
last rite — your own — willingly, weeping, honoring the soul no god ever claimed.** The most quietly
shattering scene in the game.

## Approval & fates
- **Gains:** honoring the dead, mercy to the helpless, choosing accountability over comfort, admitting
  doubt. **Loses:** desecration, cruelty, glib nihilism, mocking faith *or* doubt.
- **Fates:** **First High Doomguide of the rewritten Law** (best); a brittle keeper of the old faith; a
  heretic wanderer laying the dead outside any church; or — if she falls — an **Unbound you must lay
  yourself**, the cruelest mirror the game offers.

## Key relationships
- **[Aldric Morn](aldric-morn.md)** — her fallen mentor; the lighthouse that went dark.
- **[Sister Vayle](sister-vayle.md)** — what Garrow's doubt becomes if it chooses certainty over conscience.
- **Brother Halloran** — the teacher she must lay; the test at the heart of her quest.
- **[Naeve](naeve.md)** — the arcanist who needles her faith (*"what, precisely, is your god* for*?"* — and gets the game's best answer: *"For sitting with the dying when the people who love them can't bear to."*).

> Her answer to *what is a soul worth that no god claimed?* is **duty** — and her arc is the discovery that
> duty without a conscience underneath it is just obedience, and that she is allowed, at last, to choose
> her own creed.
