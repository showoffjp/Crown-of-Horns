# 🛡️ Maerin — *"Not Your Reason"*

> **Companion 6 of 7.** The girl from the Wall — the dead daughter of the central villain, but a **person
> first**, and the game never lets you forget it. Her arc is the game's answer **"refusal"**, and her fate
> is *"the whole game in miniature."*

---

## Identity & build
- **Full name:** Maerin Morn.
- **Class:** Ranger (Gloom Stalker, undead-touched); dual short-swords and a shortbow, with a fading
  luminescence that lets her **step partly out of the world.**
- **Joins:** **Act II** (pulled from the Wall). **Romance:** **✗ — by design.** She is the fierce platonic
  heart; the game's *"some loves are not that kind."*

## Backstory
Maerin was a *person* before she was a symbol. Cheerful, godless, sixteen, in love with the world precisely
*because* she didn't think a god stood behind it: every sunrise was a free gift from no one, and she found
that beautiful, not frightening. She died of the **Howling Pox** in the winter that took her mother
(**Saerin**) too — and because she believed in nothing, she was walled into the Faithless beside her.

Her father's entire crusade is *about* her. Everyone — **[Aldric](aldric-morn.md)**, the Choir, even **[the
Unmade](the-unmade.md)** — treats her as a **symbol:** the innocent the Wall damned, the reason to burn the
world. You can pull her out of the Wall in Act II, and the **first thing the resurrected girl does is refuse
the symbol.** She did not die to be a war-cry. She will not be saved at the cost of a city. She is
*furious*, and luminous, and entirely her own.

## The wound
*She died for **nothing**, by the cosmos's own accounting — and now everyone wants to make her death **mean**
something, which is its own erasure.* Maerin wants to be a **person**, not a stake. And her **need** — the
tragedy at the game's core — may be to be allowed to **finish dying on her own terms.**

## The secret
She is **half-dissolved and running out of time even outside the Wall** — the dissolution doesn't stop, only
slows. And she has her own fierce opinion: that the world should **not** burn to save her. She refuses to be
her father's reason, or yours.

## Personal quest — *"Not Your Reason"* (woven through Acts II–V)
- **Ch. 1 — The Refusal (Act II):** pulled from the Wall, half-dissolved, she confronts what she's become
  and what she's *for.* She **forbids you to use her against her father.** *(You can break this promise.
  She'll know.)*
- **Ch. 2 — The Father (Act III–IV):** the most direct line to Aldric's heart — a reunion with a dozen
  branches. She can plead, rage, forgive, or coldly tell him the daughter he's burning the world for would
  rather have **stayed dead** than be his excuse. Her words can **break** Aldric (redemption), **harden**
  him (he decides the Wall corrupted her, becoming Myrkul's perfect vessel), or leave him a weeping ruin who
  proceeds anyway.
- **Ch. 3 — The Choice That Isn't Yours (Act V):** Maerin is fading no matter what; the climax is the game's
  hardest gut-punch — *you* can fight to **save** her (binding her soul to the world at terrible cost — and
  she may not thank you); **let her go** on her own terms; or — if you walk **[Jergal](jergal.md)**'s road —
  she can choose to **return to the Wall willingly**, not to be dissolved but to **anchor the rewrite**,
  freeing thousands, her death finally *meaning* something **because she chose it.** Her last line in that
  path breaks Aldric, the player, and the entire moral architecture of the game at once.

## Approval & fates
- **Gains:** honesty, treating her as **capable**, letting her **choose**, refusing to use her. **Loses:**
  condescension, sentimentality, using her against Aldric after promising not to.
- **Fates:**
  - **Saved** and given *one true year*, godless and laughing (bittersweet — she resents the cost);
  - **released gently** on her own terms;
  - **returned to the Wall by choice** to anchor the rewrite (the most devastating "victory");
  - or — the **cruelest card in the game** — preserved *forever* in the **Deathless Garden** ending:
    sixteen and smiling and **static**, loved into a padded eternity, never allowed to finish her sentence.

> **Which of those you can live with is the whole game in miniature.**

## Key relationships
- **[Aldric Morn](aldric-morn.md)** — her father; his whole crusade; the heart she can break, redeem, or harden.
- **Saerin** — her mother, walled beside her.
- **[Ilfaeril](ilfaeril.md)** — the wall-builder and the wall-swallowed, forgiving each other on behalf of everyone who can't (shared epilogue if both live).
- **[Roen](roen-alleywind.md)** — the orphan who *chose* his name and the girl who refuses her *symbol*; the party's sharpest, most tender pairing.

> Her answer to *what is a soul worth that no god claimed?* is **refusal** — the insistence that a person is
> not a stake, a war-cry, or a reason, and that even the dead get to choose what their death is for.
