# 🛡️ Naeve of the Seventh Enclave — *"Karsus's Last Theorem"*

> **Companion 4 of 7.** The mother of the first apocalypse. Her arc is the game's answer **"knowledge"**
> tested to destruction — and she is the first to say aloud the thing that re-frames everything: **you are
> a loop.**

---

## Identity & build
- **Full name:** Naeve, Arcanist of the Seventh Enclave of Netheril (no surname — Netherese of her caste
  took the city's number).
- **Class:** Wizard (Netherese High Magic flavor — evocation + chronurgy hints); the party's **artillery**
  and its **lore-engine.**
- **Joins:** **Act III** (Netheril, −339 DR). **Romance:** ✅ (cerebral; two world-enders learning to be
  carried).

## Backstory
Naeve was a prodigy in a civilization of prodigies — the flying cities of **Netheril**, where magic was so
total the powerful literally lived in the sky. She helped derive a piece of the **theorem** that the
archmage **Karsus** later weaponized into the single most catastrophic spell ever cast: an attempt to
*substitute himself* for the goddess of magic. Naeve's mathematics were clean. Karsus's ambition was not.
When he cast it, **the goddess died to stop him, the Weave failed for a heartbeat — and every flying city
in Netheril, held aloft by magic, fell out of the sky at once.**

You pull Naeve from the rubble of the Seventh Enclave **in the instant of the fall.** She has lost her
entire world, her people, and — worse for her — her certainty that **knowledge is clean.** She helped end
a world with elegant proofs, and she will never trust elegance again.

## The wound
*Her genius ended a world.* This makes her **dangerously sympathetic to the Unmade's logic** — *just undo
the mistake* — because she, more than anyone, wants a mistake undone. Her **need** is to accept that some
mistakes can only be **carried**, not erased; that a person is more than their worst correct answer.

## The secret
She has read the forbidden marginalia and **suspects the Unmade is real** — and she has seen the seam in
your soul *before*, in a proof written in a hand **identical to yours**, dated centuries before your birth.
She is the **first to suspect you are a loop.**

## Personal quest — *"Karsus's Last Theorem"* (three chapters)
- **Ch. 1 — The Surviving Pages (Act III):** fragments of Karsus's full proof survived the fall, scattered
  across the ruin and across *time.* Naeve wants them — *to understand*, she says. *To use*, you suspect.
- **Ch. 2 — The Margin (Act IV):** assembling the proof, she finds a marginal note in the theorem's oldest
  copy — in **your handwriting**, dated centuries before your birth. She is the first companion to say the
  unspeakable thing aloud: ***you are a loop.*** The scene re-frames her arc and yours at once.
- **Ch. 3 — Burn It or Cast It (Act V):** the completed theorem could give the Unmade exactly the tool it
  needs — a clean substitution, a way to *un-write* without the mess. Naeve must choose to **burn the last
  theorem unused** (accepting that her life's masterwork must die so the world can live with its mistakes),
  or **hand it over** (offering the Unmade its victory, out of a grief for her own lost world she can
  finally, ruinously, indulge).

## Romance arc
Two people who have each ended a world, circling whether they're allowed to be loved anyway. A romance of
**arguments** — long, brilliant, late-night debates about determinism and guilt that slowly stop being
about philosophy and start being about the two of them. The turn:
> *"I have spent a thousand years certain that the universe is a proof and we are all just steps in it.
> Then you did the one thing my mathematics could not predict. I do not have a word for what you are. I
> have decided the word is going to be 'mine,' if you'll permit the imprecision."*

If romanced, her best ending has her **teaching children arithmetic that builds**, the theorem burned,
finally forgiven.

## Approval & fates
- **Gains:** curiosity tempered by restraint, intellectual honesty, choosing truth over comfort, mercy.
  **Loses:** willful ignorance, reckless ambition, cruelty to the curious.
- **Fates:** **the one who talks the Unmade down** (she alone can meet its logic on its own terms); the one
  who **hands it the theorem** and watches a second sky fall, smiling, undone; or a **teacher**, at last at
  peace.

## Key relationships
- **[The Unmade](the-unmade.md)** — whose "just undo the mistake" logic she is uniquely able to meet, *and* uniquely tempted by.
- **Karsus** (& his apprentice, **Proctor Ynn**) — the Folly she helped enable; *Ynn* recognizes your hand in the margins even before she does.
- **[The Returned](the-returned.md)** — the loop she's the first to name.
- **[Sister Garrow](sister-garrow.md)** — faith vs. knowledge, in the party's most quotable banter.

> Her answer to *what is a soul worth that no god claimed?* is **knowledge** — and her arc is learning that
> the cleanest possible proof can end a world, and that some mistakes can only be carried, never solved.
