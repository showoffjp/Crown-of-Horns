# ⚖️ Sister Vayle — *the Keeper of the Worst Available Atrocity*

> Acts I–II antagonist; a **true believer, not a villain;** a possible late ally you'll despise. The
> institutional foil — defender of the Wall — and, infuriatingly, the only person telling a kind of
> truth.

---

## Role
- High Doomguide of Kelemvor's church on the Sword Coast; hunts Aldric as an apostate; detains the
  Returned; burns the harvest-village.
- Returns in Act V with the church's last desperate army — **redeemable, or not, per your Act I choices.**

## Backstory
Vayle rose through Kelemvor's church **not from cruelty but from terror disciplined into duty.** As a
novice she read the sealed archive — the **Ledger of the Unwalled** — which records every prior attempt to
reform the afterlife. She expected to find permission to hate the Wall. She found instead a **mathematics
of catastrophe** in which the Wall is, *every single time*, the **smallest** available horror:

- Breach the Wall at one niche → a plague of whispering dead.
- Judge one Faithless soul by mercy → it claims it has been judged and so must all the others, and a war of the screaming undying follows.
- Abolish the category entirely → the **Spellplague**, blue fire, a wounded world.

The Synod's conclusion, written at the Ledger's end, has been her creed ever since: ***the Wall is the
least of the available atrocities. Keep it. Tell no one why.***

So she keeps it. She hunts Aldric, detains you, **burns Hollowmere** — and every cruelty is, in her own
ledger, a smaller number than the cruelty she is preventing. She is not a hypocrite. She is the **most
honest consequentialist in the setting**, and her honesty has cost her everything tender she ever had.

## The two secrets (what makes her tragic, not merely hard)
1. **She is dying** — a wasting illness she has hidden from her order, because the church cannot afford a
   dying High Doomguide and the work cannot afford to stop.
2. **She is Faithless herself.** She lost her god decades ago, somewhere in the Ledger's pages, and has
   kept performing the rites anyway, for the church's sake, for everyone but herself. She knows — *better
   than anyone alive, with absolute certainty* — that **the Wall is waiting for her, personally**, the
   moment she dies. **And she defends it anyway.** That is either the bravest or the most broken thing in
   the game, and you get to decide which.

## The wound
A creed built from terror; a god lost and the rites kept up regardless; a death she knows ends in the very
Wall she guards. She has spent herself entirely on preventing larger horrors and has nothing left over for
herself.

## Want vs. need
- **Want:** keep the Wall — guard the worst-available-atrocity against the catastrophes the others would unleash.
- **Need:** to be told, before the end, that the thing she guards is **monstrous** *and* that her guarding
  it was, in its terrible way, a kind of **love** — and to decide which of those she can live (and die)
  with.

## Possible roles & fates
- **Ally against Aldric** while you loathe what she protects.
- **Broken / exposed** — her secrets unmade, her church shattered.
- **Witnessed** — the one person who tells her the truth about both the Wall *and* her courage. *(She will
  not thank you. She may weep.)*
- She is what the game *becomes* if you choose the **Doomguide's Peace** ending: someone who looked at the
  oldest cruelty in the multiverse, understood it completely, and defended it anyway — and was, perhaps,
  **not wrong**, which is the worst thing of all.

## Key relationships
- **[Aldric Morn](aldric-morn.md)** — her quarry; the abolitionist whose cure she has read the consequences of.
- **[Sister Garrow](sister-garrow.md)** — the Doomguide who stayed but *doubts*; Vayle is the doubt's terrifying mirror, the one who read the Ledger and chose certainty.
- **[Jergal](jergal.md)** — the eldest scribe whose abdicated truth (that the law can be *rewritten*) is the one thing that could make Vayle's whole tragic calculus obsolete.

> She defends hell forever rather than gamble heaven, because she has read exactly what gambling heaven
> costs. Tell her she is wrong, and you may be lying. Tell her she is right, and you damn the Faithless
> with her. That is the trap she lives in — and she walked into it open-eyed, and stayed.
