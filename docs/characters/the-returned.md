# 👤 The Returned — *the Protagonist*

> Player-created (any race/class/background). The fixed truth: **you die in the Prologue and are pulled
> back.** You perceive the lingering dead, and a seam runs down the middle of your soul that is, in
> truth, a seam in **time.**

---

## Role
- **Type:** the player character. Build is free; the *story-soul* is fixed.
- **Tag / flag space:** `pc.true_name`, the Nameless thread, `unmade.understood` — the lore route that unlocks the deepest endings.

## The universal backstory (true regardless of build)
You were, once, ordinary — or believed you were. The seam was always there; the harvest only widened
it. What no version of you remembers is that **this is not your first death, or your hundredth.** You
are the **First Discarded** — the very first soul the Law of the Faithless ever damned, ten thousand
years ago, pulled back by a grief that did not yet have a name.

Every life you have lived since has ended the same way: **death → the Wall → the hand that pulls you
back → the long road to a throne → a new grief seeded into the absence, which pulls back the *next*
you.** You are a closed loop wearing a different face each cycle. The game is the **one cycle** in which
you might learn this in time to *choose* differently.

## The four backgrounds — *four facets of the same wound*
| Background | The facet |
|---|---|
| **Candlekeep Acolyte** | As a child you read a chronicle that should not exist and saw, for a heartbeat, your own true name. You forgot it on purpose; recovering it is the lore route. |
| **Flaming Fist Deserter** | You died once already in a war you fled; the guilt is the seam's cover story. Contacts and a debt in the Gate. |
| **Doomguide's Ward** | You served Kelemvor's church **under Aldric Morn.** You loved him like a father. The most intimate, most painful road to the central villain. |
| **The Nameless** *(the true route)* | You woke after your death with **no past at all.** The Wall remembers you even though you don't. Your forgotten name is the master key to the whole mystery — and the slow, dreadful recovery of *who you were the first time you died* **is the recovery of the Unmade's name, which is your own.** |

## The wound
You died, and were pulled back, and cannot remember the word that did it. The seam in your soul is not
a metaphor — it is where time folds back on itself, where every prior cycle is pressing through.

## The secret (the keystone of the whole game)
**You are the loop's origin.** The Unmade is your ungrieved grief made vast; the Last Returned is the
*you* that already said yes; the unremembered word is your true name; and the first soul the first
verdict ever discarded — the soul that *became* the Unmade — was you. **There is no beginning. The loop
is you, grieving yourself across all of time.**

## Want vs. need
- **Want:** to *live* — to keep the second life that was stolen back for you.
- **Need:** to understand that the thing that keeps pulling you back is your own unfinished grief — and
  that the only act of love left to you may be to finally, truly, **stay gone.**

## The arc
You begin grasping at survival and a stolen second life, and walk — through a city, an afterlife, four
ages, and a heaven revealed as a tomb — toward the single discovery that re-frames everything you've
done: that you are not the cosmos's victim or its key, but its **author.** The ending you can reach is
the measure of what you learned to do with that.

## Key relationships
- **[The Last Returned](the-last-returned.md)** — your future self, here to "spare you the long road" by making you become it faster.
- **[The Unmade](the-unmade.md)** — your grief, made sapient; it asks not to be defeated but to be *grieved and let rest.*
- **[Aldric Morn](aldric-morn.md)** — the mentor-as-monster; if you're a Doomguide's Ward, the man you loved like a father.
- **[Maerin](maerin.md)** — the daughter you can pull from the Wall; the conscience that refuses to be your reason.
- **[The Mournlight](the-mournlight.md)** — the sliver of your future self that still wants the loop to *break*; your own grief, finally on your side.

## Endings you author
All six branches read *you* — your factions, your Crown, your name (`pc.true_name`), the Mournlight,
Jergal's keyhole. The two hardest and best — **Jergal's Keyhole** (forgive the last discarded soul *by
its true name — which is yours*) and **Break the Loop** (refuse every throne and **stay gone** so the
next might be free) — require the Nameless thread. See [`../story/04_ENDINGS.md`](../story/04_ENDINGS.md)
and [Act V](../chapters/05_ACT_V.md).

> *"You came back. They never come back."* — the whole game is the discovery of why **you** always do,
> and what it would cost, for once, to stay gone.
