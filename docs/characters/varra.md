# 🛡️ Varra — *"The Fine Print"*

> **Companion 3 of 7.** The tiefling running from her own pact. Her arc is the game's answer **"defiance"**
> tested to destruction: *she was currency before she was a person — and she has literally seen the
> number.*

---

## Identity & build
- **Full name:** Varra Tasforel — *the surname is her patron's, branded on her at the pact;* she'd burn it
  off if she could.
- **Class:** Warlock (Pact of the Chain / the Undying patron-flavor); eldritch blasts, hexes, a quasit
  familiar named **Spite;** gallows humor as a class feature.
- **Joins:** Act I. **Romance:** optional, prickly, comic-tragic.

## Backstory
Varra was **sold.** Not metaphorically — at **six years old**, her parents, drowning in debt to a
death-power's broker, signed her into a pact to clear it. She grew up knowing the **exact price of her own
existence, in coin**, because she'd seen the receipt. She is funny the way a knife is sharp: as a survival
tool. Every joke is a small refusal to be only what she was traded for.

Her patron has been **silent for years** — which is worse than loud, because it means she doesn't know
when the bill comes due. And the bill, she's begun to realize, is **circling the Crown of Horns.** Her
patron is a death-power — possibly **[Myrkul](myrkul.md)**'s own agent — and her pact may be a **leash** the
villains can yank at the exact moment you need her most. She knows it.

## The wound
*She was currency before she was a person.* Varra needs to learn she is worth **more than her price** —
which is hard, when you've literally seen the number.

## Personal quest — *"The Fine Print"* (three chapters)
- **Ch. 1 — The Silence Breaks (Act II):** the patron speaks for the first time in years — through Spite,
  through mirrors, through the mouths of the dying. It wants Varra **near the Crown.** She realizes she's
  been steered her whole life.
- **Ch. 2 — The Broker (Act III):** you track the broker who sold child-Varra — now an immortal middleman
  of soul-debts — to learn the pact's exact terms. The horror: there's an out, but it requires her to
  **sell someone else** the way she was sold.
- **Ch. 3 — Voiding the Contract (Act IV):** Varra chooses —
  - **Sell a soul** (a cruel player can talk her into it; it costs her *everything* that made her worth saving),
  - **pay the price herself** (a piece of her life, power, or memory), or
  - the hard, earned option — **prove the contract void from its origin**, because *a six-year-old cannot
    consent*, and a death-power's law, **like the Wall**, is only a law because no one ever argued it down.
    This last ties her arc directly to the game's thesis: **unjust law is just a decision waiting to be unmade.**

## Romance arc
A war of attrition against her own conviction that she's unlovable *because she's priced.* She'll test you
cruelly, push you away, assume the worst — and the romance is won not by grand gestures but by **staying**
through the tests. The payoff line, if you get there:
> *"You know the worst thing about being sold? It's not the owning. It's that some part of you agrees you
> were worth selling. You've spent a year refusing to agree. I don't know what to do with that except —
> apparently — this."*

If freed and romanced, her ending is her **burning the receipt** at last.

## Approval & fates
- **Gains:** audacity, loyalty under pressure, defying authority, gallows humor, refusing to abandon
  people. **Loses:** self-righteousness, leaving someone behind, bowing to power.
- **Fates:** **free and insufferable and finally worth more than her price**; **bound forever** (collected
  mid-game if you let the leash pull); or **hollowed out**, having sold another to save herself — alive,
  free, and unrecognizable.

## Key relationships
- **Quill** — the polite, patient, infernal pact-broker collecting her soul-debt; the worst kind.
- **Spite** — her quasit familiar and the channel her silent patron speaks through.
- **[Myrkul](myrkul.md)** — possibly the power behind her patron; the leash on the Crown.
- **[Ilfaeril](ilfaeril.md)** — her favorite sparring partner (*"Ten thousand years of guilt. You know what that is? That's a hobby, old man."*).

> Her answer to *what is a soul worth that no god claimed?* is **defiance** — and her arc is the discovery
> that an unjust contract, like the Wall itself, was only ever a decision, and decisions can be **argued
> down.**
