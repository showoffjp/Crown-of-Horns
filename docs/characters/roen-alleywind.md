# 🛡️ Roen Alleywind — *"The Song That Names You"*

> **Companion 2 of 7.** The Harper who stopped reporting. His arc is the game's answer **"belonging"**
> tested to destruction: *what does it cost to refuse the bill on the only family you've ever had?*

---

## Identity & build
- **Full name:** Roen Alleywind — *a name he chose;* no one knows the one he was born with.
- **Class:** Bard (College of Whispers) / Rogue; rapier, hand-crossbow, lockpicks; **inspiration** buffs,
  sneak attack, and out of combat the party's **face / thief.**
- **Joins:** Act I (already built into the hub). **Romance:** ✅ (banter-to-bedrock, with teeth).

## Backstory
Roen was an **orphan of Baldur's Gate's Outer City** — the slums outside the walls, where the Flaming Fist
doesn't patrol and the Guild raises its own. He survived on charm and quick fingers until the **Harpers**
found him at fourteen, and for the first time in his life something *chose* him. He'd have died for them.
He nearly has, a dozen times. They gave him a family, a purpose, and a rule he never questioned: **the
mission is the love. You don't get attached. You get results.**

Then they assigned him **you.** A Returned soul with a hole in it is, by Harper doctrine, a **security
risk** — a crack reality might leak through — and the order, when it became clear what you were, was not
*protect.* It was **use, learn what you can, and remove.** Roen filed three reports. Then he stopped. He
has been lying to the only family he's ever had for weeks, and the terror of *that* — not of the villains,
of **that** — is why he never, ever stops joking.

## The wound
*Belonging always came with a price tag, and he's about to find out what it costs to refuse the bill.*
Roen needs to let one person all the way in **and survive it** — the one thing his whole life trained him
never to risk.

## Recruitment
He's already in the hub (the green NPC) — but the *real* recruitment is the Act I moment you catch him in a
lie, and he could talk his way out, and chooses, visibly, terrified, **not to:**
> *"You want the truth? The truth is I was sent to bury you, and three weeks ago I started digging the
> wrong direction, and I have no idea who I am if I'm not a good little Harper. So. Hi. I'm Roen.
> Apparently I'm yours now. Please don't make me regret the worst decision of my life."*

## Personal quest — *"The Song That Names You"* (three chapters)
- **Ch. 1 — The Audit (Act I/II):** the Harpers notice he's gone quiet. A handler — **Maelle**, the woman
  who recruited him, the closest thing he has to a mother — comes to bring him in, or bring him down.
- **Ch. 2 — The Purge (Act III):** worse than one defection — a mole has rotted a whole Harper cell, and
  Roen's silence made him the prime suspect; the order is purging itself, and good people are dying for his
  choice. He must decide: clear his name by handing **you** over, or clear the *cell* by exposing the real
  mole — at the cost of Maelle, who's been protecting him by lying too.
- **Ch. 3 — The Name (Act IV/V):** the title pays off. Roen — who *chose* his name — finally tells you the
  one he was **born** with, the orphan name he buried, in the same scene where you're trying to remember
  **yours.** Two people who threw away their names, helping each other dig them back up. His choice mirrors
  yours: hold to the self he invented, or accept the one he was given.

## Romance arc
The classic **banter-to-bedrock** arc, done with teeth — every flirt is deflection until it suddenly
isn't. The turn comes when he stops performing, exhausted after the Purge:
> *"I've conned everyone I've ever met into thinking I'm fine. You're the first person I can't lie to, and
> it's the most naked I've ever felt, and I think that's what people mean when they say love, and I hate
> it, and don't you dare stop."*

If romanced and he survives, the ending card is simply: **an inn, two chairs, no mission**, for the first
time in either of your lives.

## Approval & fates
- **Gains:** wit, loyalty under fire, protecting the powerless, honesty about cowardice. **Loses:**
  sanctimony, cruelty to the weak, selling someone out for advantage.
- **Fates:** **Harper spymaster** (reformed the order from inside); **exile** who keeps you company at the
  end; **the one who holds your hand** in the Break the Loop ending; or — if betrayed — a Harper blade in
  the dark and a single line of regret in the epilogue.

## Key relationships
- **Maelle** — his handler-mother; the Harper family the quest forces him to weigh against you.
- **Wrenna Alleywind** — his sister, a Harper on a leash; the reason he can't go home.
- **Old Sabira** — his old fence, who holds the secret (a killing at fourteen) that could unmake him (*"The Thing in the Dark"*).
- **[Maerin](maerin.md)** — the orphan who *chose* his name and the girl who refuses her *symbol*; a sharp, tender banter pairing.

> His answer to *what is a soul worth that no god claimed?* is **belonging** — and his arc is learning that
> a self you *chose* and a self you were *given* can both be real, and that letting one person past the
> jokes won't kill you.
