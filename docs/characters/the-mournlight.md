# 🛡️ The Mournlight — *"The Mercy You Become"*

> **Companion 7 of 7 (secret).** A defector fragment of the **[Last Returned](the-last-returned.md)** — a
> sliver of your own future self that, against everything, still wants the loop to **break.** Your own
> grief, finally on your side. Its arc is the game's answer **"mercy"** — and it is the key to the two best
> endings.

---

## Identity & build
- **Identity:** a faint, mournful echo of who you are and who you'll become — the only entity in existence
  that has been on **both** ends of the loop and still chooses the end.
- **Build:** mirrors the protagonist (it *was* you); a **"shadow" unit** whose kit reshapes to echo your own
  choices.
- **Joins:** late **Act IV / Act V**, **lore-gated** — requires `unmade.understood` **+** the **Nameless**
  thread. **Romance:** ✗ — *it's you.*

## Backstory
When the **Last Returned** looks at its earlier self and feels something other than certainty — a flicker
of the mercy it has spent centuries grinding away — that flicker has **nowhere to go.** The Unmade's frozen
world has no room for change, and so the flicker is **expelled**: a piece of grief the deathless avatar
could not metabolize. It drifts back along the loop and finds **you** — the **Mournlight.**

## The wound & the gift
The Mournlight **is** the wound — your **ungrieved grief, given a voice at last.** It cannot *want* anything
(the Last Returned burned out wanting), but it can **need**, and what it needs is the thing the whole game
has been about: **an ending.** Recruiting it is the proof that even the monster you become contains the seed
of its own mercy.

## The role
The Mournlight has no quest of its own — **its quest is you.** In the final act it speaks the lines the Last
Returned *can't* — not *"become me,"* but *"you don't have to."* Its existence is the game's **gentlest
argument:** that **identity across time is not a sentence**, that the worst version of you is still *you*
enough to want you free.

## Why it matters mechanically
It is the **key that unlocks the two best endings** — **[Jergal's Keyhole](../chapters/05_ACT_V.md)** and
**Break the Loop** — because *only a piece of the loop that has seen the whole loop can show you how to
leave it.* (See [`../story/04_ENDINGS.md`](../story/04_ENDINGS.md).)

## Its one line
> *"I remember this conversation. I remember everyone in it dying. I am trying very hard to be glad I'm here
> for it again instead of just sad. That's the whole job, in the end. Being glad and sad at once, and not
> letting one eat the other."*

## Key relationships
- **[The Last Returned](the-last-returned.md)** — what it defected *from*; the certainty it could not stomach.
- **[The Unmade](the-unmade.md)** — the grief it is made of; the rest it wants to grant.
- **[The Returned](the-returned.md)** — you; its whole reason; the self it came back to set free.

> Its answer to *what is a soul worth that no god claimed?* is **mercy** — and it is the proof that even the
> thing you become can choose, at the last, to want an end.
