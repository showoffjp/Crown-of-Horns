# 🗝️ Jergal — *the Clerk Who Read the Last Page*

> The eldest dead-god; not a faction. The **keyhole** to the golden ending. He will not help you and will
> not stop you — but he holds the one piece of esoteric truth that makes the **rewrite** possible, and he
> half-hopes you'll be fool enough to use it.

---

## Role
- The neutral door. The single source of the knowledge that the Law of the Faithless can be **rewritten**
  rather than torn down — the spine of **Jergal's Keyhole**, the golden ending.

## Backstory — *the clerk who read the last page*
Before the Dead Three, before Myrkul, before the Wall had a keeper, there was **Jergal**, the **Lord of
the End of Everything** — entropy given a throne, the final silence wearing a crown. He held dominion
over all death and decay across the multiverse, and held it for an age so long that he did the one thing
no other god has ever done: **he got bored.**

Not bored the way a mortal is bored. Bored the way you are bored when you have genuinely read the last
page of the only book, found it unsurprising, and now must sit through the whole thing again and again
forever knowing exactly how it ends. Jergal looked at eternity and at his own inevitable victory — *for
entropy always wins; that is the entire point of entropy* — and found the certainty of it unbearable in
its tedium. So when three ambitious mortals came clawing for his throne, he did something that horrified
the entire pantheon: **he handed it to them.** Split his godhood three ways (creating the **Dead Three**),
kept nothing but a clerk's title and a clerk's ledger, and took a job recording the names of the dead with
the patience of a man who has nowhere he'd rather be and nothing he'd rather do, *because he has already
done and been everything.*

## The watching
He has watched **[the Unmade](the-unmade.md)** grow for ten thousand years. He felt it accrete, niche by
niche, soul by discarded soul, below the floor of the afterlife. **He said nothing. He warned no one.**
Not from malice — *malice requires caring how things turn out* — but from the one feeling he has left, the
feeling that made him give away a throne: **curiosity.** He wants to know how it ends. It is the only thing
in the multiverse he does not already know, because the Unmade, and the loop, and **you**, are the one
variable his last page did not account for.

## The keyhole
Jergal holds the single piece of esoteric truth that makes the **golden ending** possible — the knowledge
he *abdicated a throne to escape*: that the Law of the Faithless can be **rewritten** instead of broken,
**if someone is willing to do the unbearably, eternally tedious work of judging every discarded soul by
hand, one at a time, forever.** He gave up godhood precisely to avoid that work. He would find it
*hilarious* if you volunteered for it. He half-hopes you will — and the fact that a thing as old and
emptied-out as Jergal can still **half-hope** is one of the most quietly moving details in the whole game.

## Want vs. need
- **Want:** to find out how it ends. (The only desire he has left.)
- He is the rare figure with no arc and no stake — and that is exactly his function: the one perfectly
  neutral door, who offers the hardest, kindest road and then steps back to watch.

## Role in the endings
- **Jergal's Keyhole** (golden) — requires `jergal.keyhole = true` (his counsel + **[Naeve](naeve.md)**'s
  theorem + **[Ilfaeril](ilfaeril.md)**'s witness, and the **[Mournlight](the-mournlight.md)**). You
  *rewrite* the law and judge the discarded by hand — and the **last soul you judge is yours**, and you
  forgive it, and the loop, for the first time, completes as mercy instead of grief.

## Key relationships
- **[The Unmade](the-unmade.md)** — the variable he's been watching grow for ten thousand years.
- **[Myrkul](myrkul.md)** — one of the three he handed his godhood to.
- **[The Returned](the-returned.md)** — the one outcome his last page didn't predict; the soul he half-hopes will take the tedious mercy.

> *He half-hopes you'll try.* For an entity that gave away omnipotence to escape boredom, that flicker of
> hope is the warmest thing left in the oldest creature in the setting — and the door to the kindest end.
