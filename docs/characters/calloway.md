# ♠️ Calloway — *"The Honest Devil"*

> **The magnificent bastard.** The cast's **first villain who isn't sad.** Every other antagonist is
> grief-curdled — Aldric, Myrkul, the Unmade, the Last Returned, all tragic. Calloway looked at the same
> grey cosmos that discards souls and **grinned, and got rich off it.** A dead con-man and gambler who
> *beat the Wall by being too entertaining to discard* — 20 Charisma, an 11/10 aura, and almost
> impossible to hate even as he fleeces you blind and tells you, truthfully, that he's doing it. Found
> at **the Long Odds** (`play/longodds.json`, off the Night Market — the only warm, loud, well-lit room
> in the land of the dead).

---

## Identity & build
- **Name:** Calloway. *Just* Calloway — *"the first name went into a wager I lost a long time ago, and I
  never bought it back; couldn't see the profit in it."*
- **Class flavor:** the platonic ideal of a **Bard (College of Glamour) / Rogue (Mastermind)** — but
  he never fights; his weapons are charm, information, and the perfectly-arranged truth.
- **Role:** a **recurring antagonist-of-circumstance**, not a boss — you can't kill him, only out-charm
  him or walk away. **Working against you** (he wants to deal you, indebt you, broker your loyalty),
  but never your enemy in a way you can punch. **Romance:** ✗ (he'd flirt; he flirts with *everyone*;
  it's a revenue stream).

## The gimmick — *he never lies*
His whole con. *"Lying's for people who can't afford the truth, friend — and I am **obscenely** rich."*
He tells you, to your face, exactly how he's going to fleece you, because **the truth, fanned out just
so, is the prettiest hand at the table** — and because never lying lets him be asked anything, agree to
anything out loud, and still win. The Auditor's warning is the key to him: *"you brace for a liar and
you get a man who tells you the truth so warmly, so completely, that you walk out having agreed to
things you'd have fought a liar over. Honesty, in the hands of a soul that charming, is the deadliest
weapon in the dead lands."* His other two laws: **the first drink's always free**, and **he honors
every wager** — *"the one thing that separates a gambler from a thief, and I am, whatever else, a
gambler."*

## The two truths (why he's impossible to hate, and genuinely a villain)
The dead-sense read finds **two facts at once that refuse to resolve into one**:

- **The predator.** He treats souls as **currency.** He meets the discarded at the moment the Wall has
  them cornered and sells them, at interest, the dignity the gods denied them for free — trapping them
  in debt so they can't move on. He owns pieces of a hundred souls and would broker the Wall, the
  world, *you*, for the right number. No cruelty in it — *that's almost the worst part* — only
  appetite, vast and amused and remorseless.
- **The magnificent refusal.** He was discarded too — godless, unclaimed, thrown toward the Wall to
  dissolve. And somewhere in the cold he looked at a cosmos that weighed him and found him worth
  *nothing*, and **decided to simply disagree** — to *claim himself* since no god would, set his own
  price, and make it exorbitant. He is the **one soul in all the grey who flatly, gleefully refused** to
  be small, to be sad, to grieve. He looked at the Wall of the Faithless and threw a *party.* He is a
  predator, and he is also the only soul in the afterlife **who is not afraid** — and those are both
  entirely true, and that is the exact reason he's impossible to hate.

> His answer to *what is a soul worth that no god claimed?* — and it's the cast's first one that isn't
> grief: **"Whatever it has the nerve to charge. The gods discard you for free, friend. The Wall
> dissolves you for spite. I'm the only power in creation that looks at a thrown-away soul and says
> *name your price* — and one of those is a kind of love. A terrible, compound-interest,
> the-house-always-wins kind. But the only kind this cosmos left on the table."**

## How you play against him (reactive)
You **cannot beat Calloway with force** — only these:
- **The Deal** — he offers a real boon (a marker that opens a door your locked Returned's road *will*
  need) for an unspecified favor, his call. You can **haggle** (Insight): **crit** — you out-deal him so
  thoroughly he tears up the debt and *owes you one instead* (`cal.calloway_owes_you`), the most
  expensive thing he's given away in four hundred years; **fail** — he answers every question truthfully
  and charms you past the hook anyway; **fumble** — you out-smart *yourself* into a worse deal and he's
  *delighted* (`cal.signed_the_worse_deal`). Or take it eyes-open, or decline.
- **The Wager** — cut the cards, soul to soul (Deception/Charisma). **Crit:** you call the turn and make
  it true, the only soul in 400 years to beat the house straight, and he hands you a wine-red marker
  with his own name on it; **fumble:** you try to *cheat Calloway, at cards, in his own house* — *"it's
  like watching a kitten try to mug a wolf"* — and owe him double. Win or lose, he honors it.
- **The Naming** ([RETURNED]) — call him the Wall-with-a-smile to his face, and **he loves it** (the
  magnificent-bastard hallmark — he's flattered, not wounded): *"I'm having that engraved, over the
  door, in gold."* He agrees to every word, then shows you the card you didn't turn (his seductive
  logic).
- **Walking clean** — take no deal, no wager, not even the free drink. It's the one move that genuinely
  *moves* him: in four hundred years *everyone played*, and a soul that wanted and walked anyway is the
  rarest hand he's ever seen folded. He respects it so much he becomes something almost like an ally —
  *"when the world's breaking, you'll remember there's a warm room with your name un-marked in the
  ledger."*

## The court around him (the supporting cast that proves the charisma)
- **the Mortgaged Man (Em)** — *the victim who'd do it again.* A soul faded to gaps, who sold his face,
  his years, his name to escape the Wall, owes more than he is, and **defends Calloway** — because the
  Wall would have dissolved him for *free*, and Calloway, alone, *came*, and *brought a menu.* The
  [RETURNED] names the **false choice** (dissolve-for-nothing vs. be-owned-forever was never a choice;
  he deserved a third door no one built) — and wakes him to the con *under* the con: *the whole grey
  house, gods and Wall and Long Odds alike, quietly agrees a soul like him has to pay to stay.*
- **the Auditor** — *the nemesis who's losing.* Sent by Kelemvor's Concord to shut Calloway down; three
  months and eleven injunctions later, he's framed all eleven on his wall in order of how much they
  amused him, and **she's starting to like it here** — not bribed, not cheated, simply *seen* for the
  first time in ninety grey years. The [RETURNED] hands her the only weapon that works: **walk out
  owing him nothing, having drunk nothing — the one audit he can't frame.** She files the unsayable true
  report (*"recommend the order audit itself"*) and leaves unbeaten, because she stopped playing the
  game he wins.

## Why he belongs (the design gap he fills)
The roster was **all tragedy.** A cast needs the villain who's *fun* — the Dio/Barbossa/Hades/Loki who
gets the best lines, who you root for despite yourself, whose evil is grand and gleeful rather than sad.
Calloway is also **thematically load-bearing**: he is the **capitalist of grief**, monetizing the exact
discarding the whole game indicts — the Wall's own logic, *taken honest*, with a smile and a free drink.
His most dangerous offer isn't a deal; it's the seductive idea that **the system is unbeatable so you
might as well profit** — and the game lets you be tempted, because he's so charming, and his point is so
nearly *right.*

## Key relationships
- **The Wall of the Faithless** — his mirror and his business model: *"the Wall of the Faithless with a
  good coat and a better smile."* He is what the Wall would be if it had *style* and *charged retail.*
- **[The Unmade](the-unmade.md)** — his opposite number: where it would *unmake the world* to avenge the
  discarded, Calloway *prices* them and profits — and he is the one soul in the grey not the least bit
  afraid of it.
- **[Myrkul](myrkul.md)** — a past client (Calloway will, with relish, neither confirm nor deny brokering
  for *"a very important, very pale gentleman"*).
- **The Lady in the Margins** — the one player at a higher table than his; the two of them have a long,
  unfinished, mutually delighted game running in the white space of the world.

> His one line, the whole of him: *"I am going to fleece you blind, cold one — and you are going to
> thank me for it — and that is the only honest transaction left in this entire grey swindle of an
> afterlife."*
