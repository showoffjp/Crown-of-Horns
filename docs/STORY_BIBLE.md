# THE CROWN OF HORNS — Story Bible
### *A Time-Spanning Forgotten Realms Epic · D&D 5e · in the lineage of Baldur's Gate II & Planescape: Torment*

> 📚 **This page is the quick reference.** The full novel-grade bible — complete synopsis, deep
> character backstories, act-by-act breakdowns, all six endings, and the esoteric lore &
> philosophy — lives in **[`story/`](story/README.md)**.

> Working title. A single-protagonist, party-based isometric CRPG that **begins** on the
> Sword Coast (c. 1486 DR) and **becomes** a journey across the hinge-points of Realms
> history. This bible is canon for the world, the cosmology, the cast, the eras you walk,
> the villains, the twists, and the endings.
>
> **IP note:** 5e *mechanics* are open (SRD 5.1, CC-BY-4.0). The Forgotten Realms
> *setting* (deities, Netheril, the Wall of the Faithless, Baldur's Gate, the Crown of
> Horns) is Wizards of the Coast's. Perfect for a personal/prototype build; a commercial
> release needs a license or original renames. Everything is data-driven — reskinning is
> trivial. The central *plot, characters, and the Unmade* are original to this project.

## 0. THE PITCH, IN ONE BREATH

The afterlife of the Realms mortars soulless dead into the **Wall of the Faithless** and
dissolves them into nothing. But *nothing accumulates.* Across ten thousand years, every
unmade soul has pooled beneath the planes into a vast, patient absence that has finally
woken up — **the Unmade** — and it wants to end the cruelty that birthed it by **unwriting
time itself**, back past the moment death's law was set, even if that unwrites all of
creation. It pulled one Faithless soul back from oblivion to use as its key: **you.**

But pulling you back gave you the one thing it cannot model — **a will of your own.** Now
you walk the cracks it tore in time — into fallen **Netheril**, the elven **Crown Wars**,
the **Time of Troubles**, the **Spellplague** — racing a force outside time, a parasite god
riding a grieving prophet, and a hunter from your own future who has already lost.

**The question the whole game asks (Planescape-style):**
> *"What is a soul worth, that no god ever claimed?"*

**Themes:** the injustice of the gods; whether an evil so old it looks like law can ever be
torn down without becoming a new tyranny; free will inside a deterministic cosmos; grief as
a force that bends history; what you owe the dead, the forgotten, and the not-yet-born.

## 1. COSMOLOGY — the three laws this story breaks

Everything hangs on three Realms truths the plot puts on trial.

1. **The Law of the Faithless.** When mortals die, the **Fugue Plane** receives them;
   Kelemvor (god of the dead) judges. Souls who served *no* god — the **Faithless** — are
   built into the **Wall of the Faithless** and slowly dissolved to nothing. This is older
   than Kelemvor; older than the gods who came before him. *No one wrote it. It simply IS.*
2. **The Weave.** All magic flows through **Mystra's** Weave. The Weave has *died and been
   reborn* — Mystryl → Mystra → Mystra again — each death a wound in the world (Karsus's
   Folly; the Spellplague). Time, in the Realms, is itself partly a structure of the Weave.
3. **The Wheel of the Dead-Gods.** Death has had many masters — **Jergal**, who gave up
   godhood from sheer boredom; **Myrkul**, slain in the Time of Troubles; **Cyric**;
   **Kelemvor** now. The throne of death is a chair anyone might seize — which is the lie
   that lures every villain in this story.

**The secret beneath all three:** the dissolved Faithless do not become nothing. *Nothing
is the one thing the multiverse cannot make.* They become **the Unmade** — an accreted
absence, a counter-creation, sapient and grieving and vast, lodged below the Fugue Plane in
the place the gods refuse to name. It is the protagonist of its own tragedy.

## 2. THE PROTAGONIST — "the Returned"

Player-created (race/class/background/abilities, full 5e). Fixed in one way: **in the
prologue you die, and you come back.** You stood in the Fugue, you saw the Wall with your
name already mortared into it — and you were *pulled out.* You are **Returned**: you
perceive spirits, the recently dead linger near you, and you have a crack in your soul that
is, you will learn, **a crack in time.**

You are the Unmade's key — but you are also the only soul in existence it did not script.
Every other piece on the board (Aldric, Myrkul, even the gods) is, to it, predictable. You
are the variable. That is why everyone — the dead-gods, the Harpers, the Unmade, and a
hunter from your own future — wants you.

**Backgrounds (each = unique dialogue + a starting contact + a thread into the deep plot):**
- **Candlekeep Acolyte** — you read a forbidden chronicle as a child; you have *seen the
  Unmade's name* and forgotten it.
- **Flaming Fist Deserter** — a soldier's contacts in Baldur's Gate; a debt that follows you.
- **Doomguide's Ward** — you served Kelemvor's church *under Aldric Morn*; the highest-
  intimacy path with the central villain.
- **The Nameless** *(Planescape route)* — you woke after your death with **no past at all.**
  The Wall remembers you even though you don't. Your forgotten name is the master key, and
  the whole game is the slow, terrible recovery of *who you were the first time you died.*

## 3. THE VILLAINS — four masks over one face

> The best villains are right about the wound and wrong about the cure. Peel each mask and
> find the next. The deepest one is *you.*

### MASK I — ALDRIC MORN, the Doomguide who said *no* *(Act I)*
For forty years the Sword Coast's finest **Doomguide of Kelemvor** — clergy who give the
dead their due. Then his godless daughter **Maerin** died and he watched his own god's law
mortar her into the Wall. He broke. Now he hunts the **Crown of Horns** (the relic holding
slain **Myrkul's** residual divinity) to *become* the god of death and tear the Wall down.
He brings you tea. He has counted every life his crusade will cost and will answer for each.
**He is not wrong about the Wall.** That is the knife. He recruits *true believers* — the
grieving, the doubting Doomguides, the Faithless who fear their own end. No one is duped.

### MASK II — MYRKUL, the Crown-Voice *(Acts II–III)*
The Crown of Horns is not a tool. It is **Myrkul**, patient and parasitic, *letting* Aldric
do the work so the cruelest of the old dead-gods can ride a sympathetic martyr back to
godhood. Aldric the abolitionist is the mask Myrkul wears to walk into the City of Judgment.

### MASK III — THE UNMADE, the absence that grieves *(Acts III–IV)*
Beneath even Myrkul: the true mover. The accreted nothing of every dissolved Faithless soul,
awake and sorrowing. It does not want power. It wants the cruelty that made it to have
**never happened** — and the only edit large enough is to *unwrite time* back before the Law
of the Faithless was set. It pulled you back to be the key that walks history. It is, in the
most literal sense, made of everyone the cosmos threw away — and it speaks with all their
voices at once. **You cannot simply defeat it; its grievance is just.** You can only answer it.

### MASK IV — THE LAST RETURNED, the you that already lost *(Acts IV–V)*
The existential gut-punch. In a timeline that already burned, *another you* said yes to the
Unmade, walked time, abolished death — and became the Unmade's avatar, a hollow tyrant who
ended grief by ending *change.* That future-self now hunts your present-self across the eras,
not from malice but from **certainty** — it remembers being you, remembers every choice you're
about to make, and believes it is sparing you the longer road to the same throne. Every era
you visit, it has been there first. The final confrontation is you, versus the proof that
you become the thing you're fighting. *What can change the nature of a Returned soul?*

### THE INSTITUTIONAL FOIL — Sister Vayle, Defender of the Wall
High Doomguide of Kelemvor on the Sword Coast; the visible opposition of Acts I–II and a
*true believer*, not a villain. She hunts Aldric, detains you, and will burn villages to keep
the Crown buried — because the sealed archives told her the truth: **every prior attempt to
reform death loosed something worse.** She would rather guard an atrocity than gamble the
world. You can ally with her while loathing what she protects — the BG2 "lesser evil."

### THE NEUTRAL DOOR — Jergal, the bored scribe of entropy
The eldest dead-god, who *gave up* godhood because eternity bored him. He is not a faction;
he is the keyhole to the third path. He has watched the Unmade grow for ten millennia and
said nothing, because he is curious how it ends. He lies only by omission, and grieves
nothing — which is its own quiet horror.

## 4. THE ERAS YOU WALK — time travel through Realms history

The crack in your soul lets the Unmade pull you (and your party) to the hinge-points where
death, magic, and time were set or shattered. Each era is a self-contained "act-region"
(hub + dungeons) with its own cast, look, and moral problem — and each is a place the Unmade
is trying to *edit*, with the **Last Returned** already at work ahead of you.

| Era (DR) | Catastrophe | What's at stake there | The surreal/existential beat |
|---|---|---|---|
| **~1486** Sword Coast | The Second Sundering / Aldric's harvest | The present; the Crown of Horns; your death | You meet the dead who linger near you; the Wall has your name |
| **-339** **Netheril** | **Karsus's Folly** | A mortal arcanist seizes the goddess of magic — and the Weave *dies* | You stand in a flying city the instant magic fails and it falls from the sky. The first "mortal-becomes-god" sin — Aldric's, ten thousand years early |
| **~-10,000** The **Crown Wars** | The Descent; the first mass-damnation of the "faithless" elves | Where the Law of the Faithless was *first enforced* at scale | A betrayal so old it became law; the origin of the Wall's cruelty |
| **1358** The **Time of Troubles** | Gods made mortal; Myrkul slain | The Crown of Horns is *forged from Myrkul's skull* — you can be present | Gods bleed in the street; you watch a god die and a chair come empty |
| **1385** The **Spellplague** | Mystra murdered; the Weave collapses; blue fire | Time is thinnest here; the Unmade nearly wins | Reality runs like wet ink; two worlds bleed together; causality optional |

> Design note: you do **not** visit these in strict order. The Unmade scatters you; piecing
> the *order of events* together — and realizing your "future" memories are another self's —
> is part of the mystery. A late revelation: some "past" companions remember **you** being
> there before you've gone, because the Last Returned wore your face.

## 5. COMPANIONS — seven, across time

Each: a 5e build, a **secret**, a **personal quest** that detonates mid-game, an **approval
axis** (`companion.<id>.approval`), a stance on the central question that can *shift*, and at
least two romanceable, one able to leave or betray you. Three are present-day Sword Coast;
three are **time-displaced**; one is the secret.

1. **Sister Garrow** — *Cleric of Kelemvor (present).* Stayed when Aldric fell; agrees the
   Wall is monstrous, fears his cure damns more than it saves. Your living rebuttal to the
   villain. **Secret:** she's terrified her faith is just cowardice. *Romance: slow, weighty.*
2. **Roen Alleywind** — *Bard/Rogue, Harper (present).* Charming Baldur's Gate fixer who
   "recruits" you. **Secret:** the Harpers ordered him to use and discard you — a soul with a
   hole in it is a security risk — and he stopped reporting. *Romance: banter turning sincere.*
3. **Varra** — *Tiefling Warlock (present).* Acid-tongued, hiding from her own patron.
   **Secret:** her patron is circling the Crown — her pact may be a leash the villains can yank.
4. **Naeve of the Seventh Enclave** — *Wizard, NETHERILE arcanist (time-displaced).* You pull
   her out of the instant Karsus's Folly kills the Weave; she watched her flying city fall.
   **Secret:** she helped *build* the spell that became Karsus's Folly — she is, in a sense,
   a mother of the first apocalypse, and she has read enough to suspect the Unmade is real.
   The Quill/Lyseira "scholar who knows too much" role, now from 10,000 years ago.
5. **Ilfaeril** — *Ranger/Paladin, elf of the CROWN WARS (time-displaced).* Survived the first
   mass-damnation of his people; remembers when the Law of the Faithless was a *choice* lords
   made, not a law of the cosmos. **Secret:** he was one of the lords who *voted for it* — and
   has spent millennia in the Fugue's edges atoning. Living proof the Wall is a *decision.*
6. **Maerin** — *Ranger / undead-touched (the stakes, embodied).* Aldric's dead daughter; you
   can pull her **out of the Wall** mid-game. She does not want to be his excuse, or yours.
   **Secret:** she is half-dissolved and fading; and she has *her own* opinion about whether
   the world should burn to save her. The most direct line to Aldric's heart. *No clean win.*
7. **(SECRET) the Pale Companion** — the **Crown-Voice** itself (Myrkul), riding along once you
   carry the relic; reasonable, monstrous, the Planescape philosopher who is also a final enemy.
   Late game, a *second* secret companion emerges: a fragment of the **Last Returned** that
   defected — a piece of your future self that chose, this once, to help you not become it.

## 7. ACT STRUCTURE — Prologue + five acts

### PROLOGUE — "What the Dead Owe" (Baldur's Gate → the Fugue, briefly) — *built ✅*
You die in Aldric's first harvest beneath the Gate, glimpse the City of Judgment and the
Wall, and are wrenched back — Returned. Recruit your first companion. **Dungeon:** the
**Cinderhaunt** beneath the Gate, crawling with the freshly **Unbound**.

### ACT I — "The Gilded Gate" (Baldur's Gate) — *partly built 🚧*
The chase begins: Aldric, Vayle, and you all hunt the Crown's location. Recruit Roen, Varra,
Garrow. Learn the Choir is full of true believers (the easy-villain frame cracks). First big
**Unbound** battle at a harvest-site; Vayle burns a village to deny you the trail — her
"lesser evil" shown whole. **Act climax:** you touch the Crown's wake and the world *skips* —
your first uncontrolled slip through time.

### ACT II — "The Book and the Wall" (Candlekeep + the Fugue Plane)
Lore + infiltration. Candlekeep yields **the turn**: the Crown is Myrkul, *and you are the
crack in the ritual.* Descend to the City of Judgment; pull **Maerin** from the Wall. Companion
quests detonate. **Act climax:** confront Aldric — he is unshaken or shaken-worse — and the
Unmade *speaks to you for the first time,* and pulls you out of your own time entirely.

### ACT III — "The First Apocalypses" (Netheril, -339 → the Crown Wars, -10,000)
The time-travel act. Walk **Karsus's Folly** as the Weave dies and a city falls from the sky;
recruit **Naeve.** Walk the **Crown Wars** where the Law of the Faithless was first enforced;
recruit **Ilfaeril.** Everywhere, the **Last Returned** has been first — and an era-companion
remembers *you* doing things you haven't done. Learn the Unmade's true nature and grief.
**Act climax:** the Unmade offers its bargain — *help me unwrite the Law, and your daughter,
your dead, every Faithless soul, are saved.* And you meet the **Last Returned** face to face.

### ACT IV — "Where the Ink Runs" (the Time of Troubles, 1358 → the Spellplague, 1385)
Reality's thinnest seams. Witness the forging of the Crown of Horns from Myrkul's skull; be
present at **Mystra's murder** as the Weave collapses and time itself becomes negotiable. The
Last Returned makes its case by *showing* you its world — beautiful, deathless, and utterly
still. Companions take sides on the central question; one may die or leave for good.
**Act climax:** the loop nearly closes — you must choose what kind of fixed point you'll be.

### ACT V — "The Crown of Horns" (the City of Judgment, all eras converging)
Convergence across time. Aldric, Myrkul, the Unmade, Vayle, Jergal, and the Last Returned all
arrive at the **Court of the Dead.** Pick your alliances; seek Jergal's keyhole. Your Returned
soul reaches its crisis — the finale *is* the resolution of your half-death, across every
era you've walked. **Finale dungeon:** the Court reconfigures by your alliances, which eras you
"edited," whether you carry the Crown, Jergal's word, Maerin, or the defector-fragment of
your future self.

### ENDINGS (six+, mix-and-match by GameFlags)
1. **Abolition-by-Atrocity** — help the Unmade unwrite the Law. The Wall ends; every Faithless
   soul across all of history is saved; the cost is oceans of the living and the unbinding of
   death. Beautiful. Wrong. The Last Returned smiles, because now you *are* it.
2. **The Doomguide's Peace (status quo)** — side with Vayle/Kelemvor. Aldric falls, the Wall
   stands, death's order endures, and you carry that you defended an atrocity to spare the world.
3. **Jergal's Keyhole (the golden third path)** — with Jergal, Naeve, and Ilfaeril, *rewrite the
   Law honestly* — the Wall ends without a tyrant's crown, paid for in truth instead of blood.
   The hardest ending to reach; requires understanding the Unmade rather than beating it.
4. **The Returned Throne** — take the Crown yourself; branch by every choice from a merciful new
   judge of the dead to the next thing that must be thrown down.
5. **Break the Loop (the existential ending)** — refuse the Unmade *and* refuse to become the Last
   Returned, even though it costs you the power to save your own dead. You die — truly, finally,
   the second death the Wall promised — so that the *next* Returned might be free. The Planescape
   ending: you change the nature of a soul by *choosing* its end.
6. **Mortal Measure** — refuse all of it; spend your borrowed time on the small human good in
   front of you, and let gods and absences be. Quiet, costly, earned.

Each ending carries per-companion and per-era epilogue slides keyed off flags, BG2-style.

## 8. THE TWISTS (spoiler ladder — reveal in this order)

1. The "easy villain" Aldric is **right about the Wall**, and his followers volunteered.
2. The Crown of Horns is **Myrkul**, riding Aldric.
3. **You are the crack in the ritual** — your second death is the key.
4. Your soul's crack is a crack in **time**; you can walk the eras.
5. The real mover is the **Unmade**, made of every discarded soul — and its grievance is *just.*
6. Your "future memories" / déjà vu are another self's: the **Last Returned** wore your face
   through history. You have been the villain of your own story the whole time.
7. **The Nameless background reveal:** your forgotten first name is the Unmade's true name —
   because the first Faithless soul it ever pulled back, ten thousand years ago, was *you.*
   You are not its key. You are its **origin.** The loop has no beginning. *That* is the horror,
   and the choice in Ending 5 is the only way to give the loop an end.

```
  text: "You were thrown away. So were we. So was everyone who knelt to nothing and was

NODE unmade.first.1  (speaker: The Unmade)
  text: "Walk back with us. To Netheril. To the Crown Wars. To the first cruelty. Pull the
         thread, and the Wall unravels in every age at once. Your daughter. Your dead. All of
         them. Saved. We ask only what was already taken from you: an ending."
  choices:
    1. "Every soul saved — and every living one in history erased. No."   -> unmade.spar
         effects: ADD companion.garrow.approval +5
    2. [INSIGHT DC 18] "You're not asking. You're remembering. We've done this."  -> unmade.loop
         (Wisdom DC18, fail -> unmade.deflect)
    3. "Show me Netheril. Show me where it started."   -> era.netheril.enter
         effects: SET act3.unlocked = true

         conditions: REQUIRE bg.nameless == true
```

Texture target: a villain whose grief is unanswerable, choices that cost across *millennia*,
and a slow dawning that the monster has been wearing your face since before history began.
