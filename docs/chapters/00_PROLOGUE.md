# 🕯️ PROLOGUE — *What the Dead Owe*

> Tutorial by trauma. You die, see the Wall, are pulled back, and cut your way out. The one hour that
> teaches the player the worst thing the cosmos does to the forgotten — and reveals that your own name
> has already been reserved on it.

---

## At a glance
- **Era / place:** Baldur's Gate undercellars → **the Cinderhaunt** (the harvest-site) → a glimpse of the **Fugue Plane.**
- **Mood:** claustrophobic; wet stone, candle-smoke, dread.
- **Length:** ~1 hour.
- **Status:** ✅ **Built** as the playable vertical slice (`PrologueBootstrap`: create a hero → dialogue → battle → XP).

---

## Premise
You are nobody in particular — a Returned-to-be living an ordinary life in the Gate's lower wards —
until the night the harvest takes you. The cult that calls itself the **Choir of the Unwalled** is
bleeding soul-light out of the living in the undercellars, and you are in the wrong cellar. The whole
chapter is a single, brutal lesson delivered before any of it is explained: *you die, you see the
Wall, you are pulled back, and you fight your way out a changed thing.*

---

## The story, beat by beat
The night begins ordinary and goes wrong fast. The Choir's harvest is underway beneath the city —
candles, chanting, soul-light drawn out of the living like marrow — and you stumble into it. There is
no clever escape. **You die.** Hard cut.

You *wake* in the **Fugue Plane**, the grey sorting-ground of the dead. You see the **City of
Judgment.** And you see the **Wall of the Faithless** — the living mortar of every godless soul,
packed in and slowly dissolving — and mortared into it with a thousand others, a space with **your
name on it**, half-formed, waiting. This is **The Reservation**: a silent, playable walk along the
Wall in which the player finds their own reserved niche *before a single word of lore explains what
they are looking at.* The dread is pre-verbal and total.

A voice "like a cathedral full of weeping" speaks **one word** over your corpse back in the living
world — a word you will spend the entire game failing to remember. And then something reaches into
the Fugue and **tears you back out.**

You return to your corpse in the cellar as it draws breath again. The Choir's herald — a masked
zealot — stares at you in something close to terror: ***"You came back. They never come back."***

You are now **Returned.** You perceive the freshly dead lingering at the edges of rooms; a cold crack
runs down the middle of your soul that aches when the world is thin. The harvest has not just killed
and revived you — it has *widened a seam that was always there.*

The chapter ends in motion: the **Cinderhaunt escape.** The harvest-site is now boiling with the
**Unbound** — souls the harvest tore loose, come back *wrong* — and you cut your way out (first
combat, first companion), staggering into the city marked, hunted, and alive.

## Key beats / "quests"
- **The harvest** — the wrong cellar, the cult, the bleeding of soul-light.
- **Your death** — non-optional, the inciting wound.
- **The Fugue vision** — the Wall, your reserved name, the unremembered word.
- **Waking Returned** — the herald's terror; the new perception of the dead.
- **The Cinderhaunt escape** — first combat (against the Unbound), first companion.

## Set-piece — *The Reservation*
A silent, playable walk along the Wall in the Fugue where you find the niche with your **own name
half-formed in it** — before anything is explained. The game's thesis stated as an image, not a
sentence: *nothing is a real place a soul can be sent, and you have already been promised to it.*

## The major development
**You are Returned** — the world has a crack in it that only you can fall through.

## The twist (seeded, not yet revealed)
The single **word spoken over your corpse** is your **true name** — and recovering it is the spine of
the whole mystery (the Nameless route). You do not know this yet. Neither does anyone who does know
choose to tell you.

## Companion beats
The first companion is recruited in the Cinderhaunt escape (in the built slice, the Doomguide
**Sister Garrow** is the earliest ally — the one who *didn't* follow Aldric and tends the Unbound).

## The villains this chapter
The **Choir of the Unwalled** appear as the immediate threat — masked, sincere, terrified of you.
Their herald flees and, unwillingly, points you toward the man who runs them: **Aldric Morn.** Behind
them, unseen, the larger powers (the Crown/Myrkul, the Unmade, the loop) are already moving — it was
*they* who seeded the harvest that took you.

## Cliffhanger
*"They never come back."* The herald's terror is the hook: a Returned soul is a thing the cosmos is
not supposed to permit. The trail points to **Aldric Morn** — and to a city full of people who will
want to use, contain, or bury you.
