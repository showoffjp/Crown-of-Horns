# 📚 ACT II — *The Book and the Wall*

> From scholarly mystery to cosmic vertigo. You buy your way into Candlekeep, find the two truths that
> turn the whole game, descend *alive* into the afterlife, pull a girl out of the Wall — and hear, for
> the first time, the voice of the true antagonist: grief itself, made vast and sapient and **correct.**

---

## At a glance
- **Era / place:** **Candlekeep** (the great library-fortress; hushed, lore-dense, *Planescape* register) → the **Fugue Plane / City of Judgment** (grey eternity, the Wall, Kelemvor's silent court).
- **Mood:** scholarly hush → cosmic vertigo; the floor of the world going thin.

---

## Premise
Find the truth of the Crown. Pay the price of entry to Candlekeep, then descend, *alive*, into the
afterlife itself — and discover both what the Crown really is and what *you* really are.

---

## The story, beat by beat

### The Book It Doesn't Have
Candlekeep admits no one without a gift: a book it does not already hold. Winning that toll is a
multi-path side-epic in itself — steal it, write it, *become* it, or destroy the one other copy to make
yours unique.

### The Sealed Stacks — *the turn*
In the deepest stacks you find two truths, side by side:

**First — the Crown of Horns is not a tool. It is Myrkul.** The dead god, patient and parasitic, is
*letting* Aldric do the work, because a sympathetic martyr is the perfect vehicle to walk the cruelest
of the old dead-gods back into the City of Judgment and onto the throne. *Aldric the abolitionist is a
mask Myrkul is wearing — and Aldric does not know it.*

**Second — and worse — you are the crack in the ritual.** The reason the harvest could pull you back is
that your soul was *already* fractured along a seam the Choir's magic only widened. You are not just a
key. You are a **fault line in reality**, and everyone who understands what that means has been very
careful not to tell you.

### The Long Walk — into the Fugue
To prove it, you do the impossible: you descend, alive, into the **Fugue Plane** and the **City of
Judgment**, and you walk the Wall of the Faithless. There you have an **audience with Kelemvor.**

### Pull Her Out
At the lip of the Wall you find your reservation again — further formed — and beside it, you find
**Maerin.** You reach into the mortar and **pull Aldric's daughter out.** She comes back half-dissolved,
luminous, fading, and *furious* — entirely her own person. She does not want to be her father's excuse.
She does not want to be *yours.* She is the moral center of the back half of the game: the actual stake,
made flesh, with opinions.

### Companion quests detonate
- **Garrow's** old mentor, **Brother Halloran**, has risen Unbound near the Fields of the Dead — and the church *let* him be harvested, to bait Aldric.
- **Varra's** long-silent patron speaks at last, steering her toward the Crown.
- **Roen's** Harper handlers send the order: *use, learn, and remove* — and the purge that follows makes him a suspect.

### The Unmade speaks — *the midpoint*
At the Wall, the true antagonist addresses you directly for the first time — not as a monster, but as a
**million grieving voices in chorus**, the condensed *nothing* of every dissolved Faithless soul across
ten thousand years, awake and sorrowing. It wants no power. It wants the cruelty that made it to **have
never happened** — and it knows how: *unwrite the law of the Faithless at its source, walk back to the
beginning, and pull the first thread.* You are the only soul who can walk that road, because you are the
crack. **Its grievance is just.**

It reaches in. The world does not skip this time. It **tears.** And you fall out of your own century.

## Key quests
- ***The Book It Doesn't Have*** — secure entry to Candlekeep with a unique volume (multi-path).
- ***The Sealed Stacks*** — uncover the two truths: the Crown is **Myrkul**; **you are the crack.**
- ***The Long Walk*** (Fugue) — descend to the City of Judgment; an **audience with Kelemvor.**
- ***The Niche With Your Name*** — find, again, your reservation on the Wall, now further formed.
- ***Pull Her Out*** — extract **Maerin** from the Wall (recruit; the stakes made flesh).
- **Companion personal quests detonate** — Garrow's mentor; Roen's purge; Varra's leash.

## Set-piece — *The Audience*
**Kelemvor** — weary, just, *bound* — tells you plainly that the Wall is **not his law and not in his
power to end**, and that the thing growing beneath his city is older grief than even he can name. **A god
admits he is not in charge.** The floor of the world feels thin.

## The major development
**Maerin** walks at your side — luminous, fading, *furious* — and refuses to be anyone's reason.

## The twist revealed (the midpoint)
**The Unmade speaks.** The true antagonist is not a god or a relic but the **condensed sorrow of every
discarded soul** — and its grievance is *just.* It offers the road back through time, and then it
**tears** you out of your own century.

## Companion beats
- **Garrow** — learns her church baited Aldric using her own teacher as a worm on a hook.
- **Roen** — his silence makes him the prime suspect in a Harper purge.
- **Varra** — her patron breaks years of silence to steer her at the Crown.
- **Maerin** — recruited; immediately *refuses the symbol* everyone wants her to be.

## The villains this chapter
**Myrkul** is revealed (the Crown is the god); he begins riding your pack from here as the **Pale
Companion** — reasonable, dry, almost kindly, terrified of the Unmade and therefore *temporarily on
your side.* **The Unmade** is revealed as the true antagonist. **Vayle** recedes; the stakes go cosmic.

## Cliffhanger
You fall out of **1486** and land in a city in the sky, ten thousand years gone, on the worst day in
the history of magic.
