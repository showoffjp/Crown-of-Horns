## The one-breath version

It is **1486 DR** on the Sword Coast. Beneath the Fugue Plane stands the **Wall of the Faithless** —
a living mortar of every soul that died believing in no god, packed in and slowly dissolved to
*nothing*. You die in a cult's soul-harvest, glimpse your **own name reserved on the Wall**, and are
torn back out: **Returned**. The saga is the discovery of what that means — through **four nested
villains** (a grieving abolitionist → the dead god wearing him → grief-itself-made-sapient → a hunter
with your own face) to the sleep-stealing truth that **you are the loop's origin, grieving yourself
across all of time** — and the six endings that hang on the one act the loop has never seen you make.

## The emotional arc, compressed

> Ordinary life → violent death → the terror of the Wall → a mystery in a great city → a mentor who
> is a monster who is **right** → a daughter pulled from oblivion → the discovery that **you are the
> wound** → the fall out of your own time → flying cities and damned elves and a hunter with your face
> → heaven revealed as a tomb → and finally a throne room at the end of all things where you learn you
> have authored your own grief since before the world was young — and are handed the one pen the loop
> never had: **the power to write the end.**
>
> *"You came back. They never come back."* — the whole game is the discovery of why **you** always do,
> and what it would cost, for once, to **stay gone.**

### Production note
Ship **Prologue + Act I** first (the vertical slice, mostly built). Each later Act is a self-contained
"era-region" — hub + 2–3 dungeons + companion beats — buildable in isolation and threaded onto the
spine. The time-travel structure is a feature *and* a production strategy: each era is a module.
