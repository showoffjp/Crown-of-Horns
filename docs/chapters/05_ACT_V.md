# ⚰️ ACT V — *The Crown of Horns*

> Apocalypse as intimacy: the fate of all history decided in a single chamber, quietly. Every thread,
> every age, every power arrives at once — and you learn the sleep-stealing truth that **the loop has
> no beginning, and the first soul it ever discarded was you.** The choice that ends the game is the
> one act, in ten thousand years, the loop has never seen you make.

---

## At a glance
- **Era / place:** the **Court of the Dead**, beneath the City of Judgment, with all eras bleeding together.
- **Mood:** apocalypse as intimacy — the fate of all history decided in a single chamber.

---

## Premise
Convergence. Choose your alliances; seek Jergal's keyhole; recover your name; answer the absence — and
make the one move that is not already a memory.

---

## The story, beat by beat

### The Convergence
Everyone arrives at once at the **Court of the Dead:** **Aldric** (with the Crown and a full Choir);
**Myrkul** (ready to shed him); the **Unmade** (vast beneath the floor of the world); **Vayle** (with the
church's last desperate army); **Jergal** (the bored eldest scribe who gave up godhood from sheer
weariness ages ago and has watched the Unmade grow for ten millennia, saying nothing, *curious how it
ends*); and the **Last Returned** (come to close the loop). Your **Act I–IV flags decide who stands with
you** — broker, betray, or break each power.

### The two deep roads
- ***The Keyhole*** (golden path) — with **Jergal**, **Naeve**, **Ilfaeril**, and the **Mournlight**, assemble the means to *rewrite* the law rather than tear it down: judge the discarded **by hand**, one soul at a time, forever — the tedious mercy Jergal abdicated a throne to escape.
- ***The Name*** (Nameless path) — recover your **true name** (the unremembered word from the Prologue, the hand in Naeve's proof, the niche with your name) and learn you are the Unmade's **origin.**

### The final twist — the loop has no beginning
The Unmade has **no first cause.** Because the *first* Faithless soul it ever pulled back — ten thousand
years ago, before Netheril, before the Crown Wars, at the very forging of the law — was **you.** The word
spoken over your corpse in the Prologue was **your own true name.** You are not the Unmade's key. You are
not even its victim. **You are its origin.** Every Returned, in every age, has been you — pulled back,
choosing, becoming the Last Returned, seeding the grief that makes the Unmade that pulls back the first
soul that is *you.* The serpent has always eaten its own tail. There was never a beginning. There has
only ever been the loop, and the loop is **you, grieving yourself across all of time.**

### The reflection-duel
***The Last Returned*** — the climax is a confrontation whose point is **not to defeat a double, but to
refuse to become it.**

### The choice
The one act the loop cannot predict — because you have never done it. **Break** it, **complete** it,
**rewrite** it, **seize** it, or **walk away** from all of it.

## Set-piece — *The Ledger*
In the final chamber, the Wall's entire **ten-thousand-year ledger of the discarded** is laid open, and
— if you walk the golden road — you may judge them, **one soul at a time, by hand**, the tedious mercy
Jergal abdicated. It can take as long as you let it. **Each name is a story.**
> *(This is the set-piece the game's 200+ walkable, witnessed souls feed — the "Wake the World
> Withheld," the "Intake Queue," and every Sigil door are the Ledger's grief made intimate and
> rehearsable.)*

## Major development & final twist
The loop has **no beginning.** The first Faithless soul ever pulled back was **you;** the unremembered
word is your own name; every Returned across history has been you, becoming the Last Returned, seeding
the grief that pulls back the first you. **You are the author of your own sorrow, across all of time.**

## The six endings (the choice)
The Court reads your accumulated state — there is no single "ending button." The branch is gated on
**faction reputation, the Crown, your true name (`pc.true_name`), the Mournlight, and `jergal.keyhole`.**
The two best (Break the Loop, Jergal's Keyhole) are deliberately **hard to reach.**

| | Ending | One line |
|---|---|---|
| 🜍 | **Abolition-by-Atrocity** — *"The Mercy That Ate the World"* | tear the Wall down by force; the cure becomes a deathless, meaning-less stillness |
| ⚖️ | **The Doomguide's Peace** — *"The Atrocity Kept"* | defeat Aldric and the Unmade by force; the Wall stands, defended by someone who understood it completely |
| 🗝️ | **Jergal's Keyhole** — *"The Tedious Mercy"* (golden) | *rewrite* the law: judge every discarded soul by hand, forgive the **last one by its true name — which is yours** |
| 👑 | **The Returned Throne** — *"The New Tyrant, Maybe Merciful"* | seize the throne of the dead and become the fixed point you watched others become |
| 🕯️ | **Break the Loop** — *"Stay Gone"* (existential) | recover your name, refuse all thrones, and give the loop the one thing it never had: a soul choosing, for the first time in ten thousand years, to **stay gone so the next might be free** |
| 🌾 | **Mortal Measure** — *"The Small Good"* | refuse it all — Crown, Wall, loop, cause — and simply *live* your borrowed time |

## Final image
Determined by your ending — a Wall coming down stone by stone; a paradise exhaling at last; **a single
soul choosing, for the first time in ten thousand years, to stay gone so the next might be free.**

## Companion fates (read by the ending)
Garrow can become the **first High Doomguide of the rewritten law**; Naeve burns Karsus's theorem and
teaches children arithmetic that *builds*; Ilfaeril is **forgiven and at rest** (or martyred holding the
Wall open); Maerin can be saved (resentfully), released gently, or **return to the Wall by choice to
anchor the rewrite** — the most devastating "victory"; Roen, Varra each get their own slides. The
**Mournlight** — your own grief, finally on your side — is the proof that even what you become can choose,
at the last, to want an end.
