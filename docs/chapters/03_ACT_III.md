# 🌆 ACT III — *The First Apocalypses*

> The time-travel act. The Unmade scatters you across the hinge-points where death, magic, and time
> were set or shattered — and at each one, **someone has already been there before you, wearing your
> face.** Awe and grandeur and the specific dread of standing inside a catastrophe you know the ending
> of — until your "destiny" turns out to be **déjà vu of another self's choices.**

---

## At a glance
- **Era / place:** **Netheril, −339 DR** (flying cities of impossible arcane splendor) → the **Crown Wars, ~−10,000 DR** (elven high courts and the first damnations).
- **Mood:** awe, grandeur, and the dread of being inside an apocalypse whose ending you already know.

---

## Premise
The Unmade scatters you across the hinge-points it means to *edit.* Survive them, learn its nature,
recruit the souls who can meet its logic — and reckon with the hunter who keeps arriving first.

---

## The story, beat by beat

### Netheril, −339 DR — Karsus's Folly
You arrive in a **flying city** in the instant a mortal arcanist named **Karsus** casts the most
ambitious spell in history: an attempt to seize the goddess of magic and *become the god of the Weave.*
It is the original sin the whole game rhymes with — **Aldric's crime, ten thousand years early.** You
watch the **goddess of magic die** to stop him; you feel the Weave fail; **every spell in the world
winks out at once** — and you are standing in a city that flies *by magic* the instant magic stops. The
city falls out of the sky.

In the screaming ruin you pull a survivor from the rubble: **Naeve of the Seventh Enclave**, an
arcanist who helped *build* the theorem Karsus weaponized — a **mother of the first apocalypse**, and a
woman who has read enough forbidden mathematics to suspect the Unmade is real. She joins you. She also
recognizes the seam in your soul, because she has seen its signature once before: in the margins of a
proof, in a hand she swears is **yours**, written centuries before you were born.

### The Crown Wars, ~−10,000 DR
You walk the elven civil wars that birthed the drow and the first mass-damnations — the era when the
**Law of the Faithless was first enforced as a *choice***, a verdict elven lords handed down on their
enemies' souls, before it calcified into cosmic law. You recruit **Ilfaeril**, an elf paladin who
survived that first damnation — and who, you learn, was one of the lords who **voted for it.** He has
spent ten thousand years at the edges of the Fugue trying to atone. He is living proof of the game's
most dangerous idea: **the Wall is not physics. It is a decision someone made, and decisions can be
unmade.** The Unmade *adores* him — which is exactly why you should fear it.

### The hunter, always first
Everywhere, the **Last Returned** has been first. An era-companion remembers *you* doing things you have
not done. A door is already open that you were going to open. A man you were going to kill is already
dead — killed kindly, by someone who looked like you and knew exactly where his heart was.

At the climax you meet your hunter face to face — a pale, patient figure who lowers its hood and wears
**your face, aged by centuries of certainty** — and makes its case not with a blade but with a memory:
*it remembers being you*, remembers every choice you are about to make, and it is here not to kill you
but to **spare you the long road** to the throne it already sits on. And the Unmade, gently, makes its
offer plain at last: *help me pull the first thread, and your daughter — Maerin — and every Faithless
soul in every age are saved. I ask only what was already taken from you. An ending.*

## Key quests
**Netheril:**
- ***The Day the Sky Fell*** — arrive at **Karsus's Folly**; witness a mortal try to become the god of magic; feel the Weave die; survive the **fall of a flying city.**
- ***The Seventh Enclave*** — pull **Naeve** from the ruin; recruit the mother of the first apocalypse.
- ***Karsus's Last Theorem*** (Naeve's quest) — recover the math that could undo a world (and tempt the Unmade).

**Crown Wars:**
- ***The First Verdict*** — witness the Law of the Faithless enforced as a *choice*; recruit **Ilfaeril.**
- ***The Vote He Cast*** (Ilfaeril's quest) — confront the souls he damned.
- ***The Face Ahead*** — track the figure who's been everywhere before you.

## Set-pieces
- ***The Falling City*** — a desperate descent/escape as Netheril's enclave plummets, magic dead, gravity remembering.
- ***The Court of Knives*** — a Crown-War elven court where a single word damns a people, and you can change it — or learn you *can't.*

## The major development
You learn the Unmade's true nature — and that the Wall is a **decision**, witnessed by **Ilfaeril**, who
**voted for it.**

## The twist revealed
The **Last Returned** lowers its hood and wears **your face, aged by certainty.** It remembers being you.
It is not here to kill you; it is here to *spare you the long road.* And the Unmade makes its offer
plain: *pull the first thread, and Maerin — and everyone — is saved.* **Your "destiny" is revealed to be
déjà vu of another self's choices.** The future is not open. It is a *memory.*

## Companion beats
- **Naeve** recognizes your soul's seam from a centuries-old proof in your own hand — and is the first to say it aloud: **you are a loop.**
- **Ilfaeril** fears he's the Unmade's instrument (he is its first witness).
- **Maerin** meets a version of the world where her death *meant* something — and hates it.

## The villains this chapter
The **Unmade** (offer made plain), the **Last Returned** (revealed as you), and **Myrkul** (still riding
along, still afraid of the Unmade, still useful — and still waiting to betray you). **Karsus** and
**Coronal Aedyn** (who *invents* the Wall as a "merciful" sentence) are the era's tragic agents — sincere,
monstrous.

## Cliffhanger
To understand the offer, you must see where the Crown was *made* — and where the Weave died a second time.
