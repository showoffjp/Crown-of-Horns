# 🔵 ACT IV — *Where the Ink Runs*

> Reality at its thinnest. You witness the making of the very relic you carry and the murder that
> nearly let the Unmade win — and your future self stops fighting you and simply **shows you what
> victory costs.** The villain reveals heaven and lets you understand exactly why it's hell.

---

## At a glance
- **Era / place:** the **Time of Troubles, 1358 DR** (gods walking as mortals; Waterdeep under a wrong sky) → the **Spellplague, 1385 DR** (blue fire, merging worlds, optional causality).
- **Mood:** reality coming apart at the seams; beauty and horror indistinguishable.

---

## Premise
Witness the making of the relic you carry and the murder that nearly let the Unmade win — and be shown,
by your future self, exactly what victory costs. By the end you must decide, before the convergence,
**what kind of fixed point you intend to be.**

---

## The story, beat by beat

### The Time of Troubles, 1358 DR
The gods are cast down to walk Faerûn as mortals; they bleed in the streets; they can be **killed.**
**Myrkul dies** above Waterdeep — and you can be present at the precise moment his skull is taken up
and the **Crown of Horns is forged** from it. You finally understand, in your hands and your gut, what
has been riding in your pack. And the **Crown-Voice (Myrkul)** — a sly, reasonable "companion" since Act
II — drops the geniality and shows you the throne it actually wants.

### The Spellplague, 1385 DR
The goddess of magic is murdered a *second* time; the Weave collapses; **blue fire** runs across the
world; two worlds bleed together and **causality becomes optional.** This is where the Unmade nearly
wins — where the timeline is so wounded that a single pulled thread could unravel everything.

### The Deathless Garden — the villain's real argument
And it is where the **Last Returned** stops fighting you and simply **shows you its world** — the
timeline where you said *yes.* The Wall is gone. Every Faithless soul, saved. **Maerin, alive. Aldric,
at peace.** And it is **beautiful, and deathless, and utterly, perfectly still** — a world where nothing
ever ends, and so nothing ever changes, ever grows, ever *means.* A paradise that is also a held breath
that never exhales; every person you love, safe, smiling, and *static*, repeating the same perfect
moment forever. The most beautiful and most unbearable level in the game. *"This is what mercy costs,"*
it tells you, *"and I would still pay it again. So will you."*

### The price of consequence
Here a companion can **die for good**, or **leave** — the choices have weight and the game stops
protecting you from consequence. Romances reach their breaking or their blooming. **Maerin's time runs
visibly short** (the Wall's dissolution doesn't stop outside it, only slows). And you must decide,
before the convergence, **what kind of fixed point you intend to be** — and the loop *senses your choice
forming.*

## Key quests
- ***The Skull and the Crown*** — be present at **Myrkul's death** above Waterdeep and the **forging of the Crown of Horns;** the Crown-Voice drops its mask.
- ***The Second Murder*** — the **Spellplague;** the Weave collapses; the timeline is a held breath.
- ***The Deathless Garden*** — the Last Returned **shows you its world:** the timeline where you said yes.
- **A companion's point of no return** — a personal quest where they can die for good, or leave.

## Set-piece — *The Deathless Garden*
A playable walk through a paradise that is also a tomb — every person you love, safe, smiling, and
*static*, repeating the same perfect moment forever. The most beautiful and most unbearable level in the
game.

## The major development
The "good ending" the Unmade offers is **real and achievable** — and you now know precisely **why it's
damnation.** No strawman remains; there is nothing left to hate, only a grief you share and a price you
can't unsee.

## The twist revealed
What *kind* of fixed point you intend to be — the loop **senses your choice forming.** (And you learn,
viscerally, that the Unmade's victory is not a lie or a trap: it is a real heaven that is also a tomb.)

## Companion beats
- **Romances bloom or break.**
- **A companion may fall** (die for good) or leave — the game's first irreversible losses.
- **Maerin's** time runs visibly short; her fate begins to crowd out every other concern.

## The villains this chapter
**Myrkul** drops the mask above Waterdeep and shows the throne it wants — the betrayal it's been waiting
to commit is now nakedly close. The **Last Returned** makes its truest, gentlest, most devastating
argument (the Garden). The **Unmade** is on the verge of winning. **Aldric**, still convinced he is
saving his daughter, draws toward the convergence.

## Cliffhanger
Every thread — every age — converges on **one room** at the end of all things.
