# 🏛️ ACT I — *The Gilded Gate*

> A *Baldur's Gate* political thriller with a death-cult under it. Three powers want you and the Crown
> of Horns; you build a party, work the city, and reach the one place that knows where the Crown lies —
> while the easy moral frame collapses under you and reality starts to skip.

---

## At a glance
- **Era / place:** **Baldur's Gate** — Upper City patriars, Lower City guilds, Outer City slums; Flaming Fist patrols; the Guild in the shadows.
- **Mood:** political thriller, intrigue, grief — a beautiful rotten city with an atrocity humming beneath it.
- **Status:** the vertical-slice target; the hub and the **Cinderhaunt** dungeon are built.

---

## Premise
Three powers want you, and the **Crown of Horns** they're all chasing. **Aldric Morn** wants the
Returned because the Choir believes you are a *key.* **Sister Vayle** wants you in a cell. **The
Harpers** want you handled. You gather your first companions, maneuver through the Gate's dukes,
Flaming Fist, and thieves, and follow the Crown's trail toward the only place that knows where it
lies: **Candlekeep.**

---

## The story, beat by beat

### The three powers
**Aldric Morn** — you meet him not as a monster but as a tired, gentle, devastating old man who
brings you tea. He was the Sword Coast's finest **Doomguide of Kelemvor** for forty years, until his
godless daughter **Maerin** died of plague and he followed a forbidden rite into the Fugue and watched
his own god's law wall her in to dissolve. He begged. Kelemvor — bound by a law older than himself —
refused. So Aldric will *become* the god of death and tear the Wall down with his hands; for that he
needs the Crown (which holds the residual divinity of the dead god **Myrkul**) and enough soul-light
to ignite it. **The horror is not that he's mad — it's that he's right about the Wall, and his
followers volunteered.**

**High Doomguide Sister Vayle** wants you in a cell. She leads Kelemvor's church on the coast, hunts
Aldric as an apostate, and will do monstrous things to keep the Crown buried — because the sealed
church archives told her what happens *every* time a mortal tries to reform death. She is the boot you
instinctively hate and, infuriatingly, the only one telling a kind of truth.

**The Harpers** want you *handled* — a soul with a hole in it is a security risk. They send **Roen
Alleywind**, a charming fixer, to recruit and quietly *contain* you. (Roen will stop reporting.)

### Building the party & working the city
You recruit **Sister Garrow** (the Doomguide who *didn't* follow Aldric, now laying the Unbound at the
city's edges), **Roen**, and the acid-tongued tiefling warlock **Varra** (introduced via a pact-collector
in the Gate). You thread the city's factions — patriars, Flaming Fist, the Guild — clear the
**Cinderhaunt** harvest-site to recover the first thread of the Crown's trail and the key beneath the
city, and chase the trail toward **Candlekeep.**

### The frame collapses
The longer you spend in the Gate, the harder the easy villain/hero frame becomes to hold. The Choir is
not a pack of fanatics — it is grieving families, doubting Doomguides, and the Faithless who fear their
own end, who **volunteered.** Aldric's case is not madness. Vayle's cruelty has a logic. By Act's end,
"who's the villain" has stopped being a clean question.

### The crack opens
At the climax, **Vayle burns a harvest-village** — *Hollowmere* — to deny you the trail, and you watch
her "lesser evil" entire, her eyes meeting yours across the fire without looking away. And as you touch
the Crown's psychic wake for the first time, **the world skips.** For three heartbeats you stand
*somewhere else, somewhen else* — in a city in the sky — then snap back, nosebleeding, alone in having
felt it. The crack in your soul has started to **open.**

## Key quests
- ***Tea with a Heretic*** — your first, disarming meeting with **Aldric.** He's kind. He's right. It's awful.
- ***The Fist and the Knife*** — navigate the Flaming Fist and the Guild; recruit **Roen.**
- ***A Devil's Arithmetic*** — **Varra**'s introduction; a pact-collector working the Gate.
- ***The Cinderhaunt*** (dungeon, built) — clear the harvest-site; recover the first thread of the Crown's trail; take the key beneath the city.
- ***Ashes of Hollowmere*** — Vayle burns a harvest-village to deny you the trail.

## Set-piece — *The Burning of Hollowmere*
You arrive as the Doomguides put a village to the torch "to stop the spread." You can save a handful —
not all. **Vayle meets your eyes across the fire and does not look away.** Her "lesser evil," entire.
(The survivors you carry out — e.g. **Sable & Wick** — return later as *people*, not weight.)

## The major development
The easy moral frame **collapses.** The Choir is full of *true believers*; Aldric's case is not
madness; Vayle's cruelty has a logic. You can no longer simply hate anyone.

## The twist revealed
At the climax, you touch the Crown's psychic wake and **the world skips** — three heartbeats in a city
in the sky, then back, nosebleeding, alone in having felt it. **The crack opens.** Reality has a door
in it, and only you can feel it.

## Companion beats
- **Garrow** — quiet grief at her own church's deeds; she watches the Hollowmere fire and something in her starts to break.
- **Roen** — stops reporting to the Harpers (the first crack in his only family).
- **Varra** — her pact tightens; the silence from her patron grows ominous.

## The villains this chapter
**Aldric** (the sympathetic abolitionist), **Vayle** (the institutional foil who is, infuriatingly,
partly right), and the **Harpers** (who want you discreetly removed). Behind all of them, unseen, the
Crown's psychic wake — **Myrkul** — and the loop begin to make themselves felt as the skips.

## Cliffhanger
The trail leads to **Candlekeep** — and the skips are getting **longer.**
