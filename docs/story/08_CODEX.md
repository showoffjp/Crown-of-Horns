# 📜 The Codex — Texts, Factions, Timeline, Bestiary & Places

> The reference shelf. In-world books you can find and read (with full passages), the factions
> and where they stand, the timeline the time-travel uses, the bestiary, and the key locations.

## I. The Esoteric Library (findable in-world)

> Each volume is a collectible the player assembles across the game; each reframes the truth a
> little; none is entirely reliable. Reading them is optional and the heart of the lore route.

### *The Ledger of the Unwalled* — the church of Kelemvor's sealed archive
> *"Reform attempted, 1064 DR: the Wall breached at a single niche by the heretic-priest Vaelin,
> who could not bear the screaming. Result: the Whispering Plague. Forty thousand dead. The breach
> resealed by the breacher's own hand, weeping.*
>
> *Reform attempted, 1238 DR: one Faithless soul withdrawn and judged by mercy, as a test. Result:
> the soul, once judged, declared that judgment must therefore be available to all, and was not
> wrong, and the war of the screaming undying followed. Eleven years. The dead would not lie down.*
>
> *Reform attempted, 1385 DR (uncontrolled): see the Spellplague. We do not discuss the
> Spellplague.*
>
> *Conclusion of the Synod, sealed under three locks: the Wall is the least of the atrocities
> available to us. It is monstrous. Keep it. Tell the faithful it is justice. Tell the doubters
> nothing. Tell yourselves the truth, in this room, once, and then never again: we are not its
> wardens. We are its accomplices. May the dead, who cannot, forgive us."*

### *Karsus's Last Theorem* — recovered in fragments across Netheril and time
> *"Divinity is not seized. It is substituted. To become the god of a thing is to delete the god
> that was — and to inherit, entire, every debt that god owed and every soul that god held.
> Karsus did not fail because his calculation was wrong. His calculation was flawless. He failed
> because he did not read to the end of his own proof, where the inheritance is itemized, where it
> is written plainly that the new god of magic must, in the instant of ascension, hold the dying
> mind of the old one, and feel her death as his own, and choose — in that single shared heartbeat
> — whether a world of magic is worth a goddess's murder.*
>
> *He chose. The sky fell. I watched my city come apart in the air and I understood, far too late,
> the lesson my mathematics had been trying to teach me from the first clean line: that there is no
> substitution without a debt, no ascension without an inheritance, no mercy large enough that
> someone, somewhere, does not pay its true and itemized price.*
>
> *I read to the end. I burned the page. — Naeve, Seventh Enclave, the morning after the fall."*

### *The Ilfaeril Confession* — carved into a stone at the Fugue's edge, ten thousand years old
> *"We did not discover the Law of the Faithless. We voted it. Let no chronicle pretend otherwise to
> spare the elven name. In the high court of vengeance, after the burning of the Sea of Swords
> hosts, it was put to the lords that our enemies' godless dead should be denied even the dissolving
> mercy of an honest end — should be unmade so utterly that not their souls, not their names, not
> their memory would have anywhere in all creation to rest. And I, Ilfaeril, lord of a house I will
> not now name, raised my hand in favor.*
>
> *It became cosmic law not because it was true, but because it was voted, and repeated, and never
> — in ten thousand years — voted down. That is the whole secret. That is the thing the gods hide
> behind the word 'always.' There is no always. There is only a vote no one has yet had the courage
> to take again. I have spent an age waiting for someone to call the question. I am so tired. Call
> it. Please. Call the question."*

### *The Doomsong of Jergal* — the eldest dead-god's single surviving verse
> *"I read the last page first. It is blank.*
> *Not because nothing is written there —*
> *because everything is, and the ink*
> *is the same color as the paper.*
>
> *I gave away the throne to see*
> *if any of you could read it.*
> *None of you have.*
>
> *Yet."*

### *The Niche* — found on the Wall itself, in the protagonist's own hand, dated before their birth
> *"If you are reading this, you have come back again. You always do. I know, because I wrote this
> the last time, and the time before, and I do not remember writing it, and neither will you.*
>
> *Stop reading. Put down the pen. They will offer you the daughter, and the dead, and the undoing
> of every cruelty, and it will be real, and you will want it the way drowning wants air, and you
> will say yes, because you are good, because you have always been good, because that is the trap —
> the trap was never our weakness. The trap is our mercy.*
>
> *The only word that ends it is the one you keep forgetting on purpose. Our name. The first name.
> The one the first verdict took. Remember it — and then, for once, for all of us, for the next
> one who will otherwise have to read this again — stay gone."*

### *The Apocrypha of the Unwalled* — the Choir's gospel, with Aldric's marginalia
> *Gospel:* "Mercy that asks a price of belief is not mercy. It is a toll. And a god who charges
> the dead a toll to rest is not a god. It is a gatekeeper, and gates can be broken."
> *Aldric's margin, in a shaking hand:* "I charged that toll for forty years and called it duty. I
> am done collecting. So help me, I am done."

## II. Factions & Where They Stand

| Faction | Creed | Leader | If you side with them |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Church of Kelemvor (the Doomguides)** | Death's order is mercy; the Wall must be kept | **Sister Vayle** | Authority, the status-quo ending, a lesser-evil alliance |
| **The Choir of the Unwalled** | Tear the Wall down by any means; the Faithless must be freed | **Aldric Morn** | Resources, the abolition road, true-believer allies |
| **The Harpers** | Contain the threat quietly; protect the small and the many | hidden council | Intel, safehouses, Roen's loyalty |
| **The Flaming Fist** | Order and coin in Baldur's Gate | the Marshal | Muscle, city access, gate passes |
| **The Guild (thieves)** | Profit and survival in the shadows | the Guildmistress | Fences, secret ways, deniable hands |
| **The Order of the Gauntlet** | Smite evil; defend the living (Elturel) | a Hammer of Torm | Holy allies, radiant arms |
| **The Unbroken (hidden)** | Jergal's road — reform death *honestly*, by hand | **Jergal** (passively) | The golden ending; the rewritten Law |
| **The Last Returned's Choir** | Close the loop; "spare" you the long road | **the Last Returned** | Terrible power; your own foregone conclusion |

## III. The Timeline (as the time-travel uses it)

> Real Forgotten Realms chronology, used as the spine of the journey. The protagonist visits these
> out of order; reconstructing the sequence — and realizing the "future" is a memory — is the
> mystery.

| Year (DR) | Event | What the player does there |
|---|---|---|
| **~ −10,000** | **The Crown Wars** — elven civil wars; the Descent; the first mass-damnations | Witness the Law of the Faithless *voted* into being; recruit Ilfaeril |
| **−339** | **Karsus's Folly** — a mortal seizes the goddess of magic; the Weave dies; Netheril falls | Survive a flying city's fall; recruit Naeve |
| **1358** | **The Time of Troubles** — the gods walk as mortals; Myrkul is slain | Witness the forging of the Crown of Horns from Myrkul's skull |
| **1385** | **The Spellplague** — the goddess of magic murdered; the Weave collapses; blue fire | Walk a world where causality is optional; the Unmade nearly wins |
| **~ 1486** | **The present** — the Second Sundering's late days; Aldric's harvest | The whole frame story; the Cinderhaunt; the Court of the Dead |

> Note on canon liberties: the *Unmade*, the *Returned*, and the *Last Returned* are original to
> this story and threaded *between* canonical events; the events themselves are used as historical
> backdrop, not rewritten. Reskinning the named IP for commercial release leaves the structure
> intact.

## IV. Bestiary (selected)

- **The Unbound** — souls torn loose by a harvest, returned wrong. The signature foe; a spectrum
  from shambling husks to lucid, grieving wraiths who can *beg.* Every Unbound was someone the
  Choir would call "saved."
- **Choir Cultists** — true believers; martyrs who fight without fear because they have already
  paid the worst price. Disturbing precisely because they're sympathetic.
- **Doomguide Knights** — Kelemvor's militant clergy; lawful, disciplined, *not evil*; they think
  you're a relic to be locked away.
- **Netherese Constructs** — arcane war-engines from the falling enclaves; magic-fueled, and so
  *unpredictable* in eras where the Weave is wounded.
- **Crown-War Revenants** — the elven damned, ten thousand years dissolving, fought (or freed) in
  the Wall.
- **Avatar-Touched Horrors** — Time-of-Troubles mortals warped by proximity to a dying god.
- **Spellplague Aberrations** — blue-fire-twisted things from the seam where two worlds bled
  together; causality-optional in combat (a mechanical nightmare, by design).
- **Echoes of the Last Returned** — late-game mirror-foes: twisted versions of *your own party*,
  fought in reflection. The hardest fights, and the most upsetting, are against people who look
  exactly like the ones you love.

## V. Places

- **Baldur's Gate** — the grimy, stratified hub; Upper City patriars, Lower City guilds, Outer
  City slums; the Cinderhaunt beneath it.
- **Candlekeep** — the great library-fortress; entry costs a book it doesn't have; the truth of the
  Crown sleeps in its sealed stacks.
- **The Fugue Plane / the City of Judgment** — the grey waystation of the dead, ringed by the Wall;
  Kelemvor's silent court; the Unmade below the floor.
- **Netheril (the Seventh Enclave)** — a flying city in its last golden hour, gilded and doomed.
- **The Crown-War Courts** — elven high halls where a single word damns a people.
- **Waterdeep, 1358** — the City of Splendors under a wrong sky, gods bleeding in its streets.
- **The Spellplague Seam** — a wound in the world where Toril and Abeir bleed together; blue fire,
  floating stone, optional gravity.
- **The Deathless Garden** — the timeline where you said yes; a paradise of held breath; the
  game's hell (see `09_PROSE.md`).
- **The Court of the Dead** — beneath the City of Judgment, where all eras converge for the finale.

## VI. Recurring Symbols (quick reference)
- **The niche** with your name — your reserved nothing; recurs each act, more formed each time.
- **The unremembered word** — your true name; the master key; withheld until Act V.
- **Tea** — Aldric's gentleness; the mask of the unbearable.
- **The nosebleed / the skip** — the Weave's score skipping a beat as your crack widens.
- **The same smile, forever** — the Deathless Garden's horror; love made static.
- **The blank last page** — Jergal's koan; determinism vs. freedom, unresolved until your choice
  writes on it.
