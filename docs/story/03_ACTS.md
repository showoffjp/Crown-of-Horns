# 🗺️ Acts & Chapters — The Structure

> Prologue + five acts. Each entry: setting & mood, the premise, key quests, the marquee
> set-piece(s), the **major development**, the **twist revealed**, companion beats, and the
> cliffhanger that pulls you to the next act. Build the **Prologue + Act I** as the vertical
> slice; the eras are the dream beyond it.

---

## PROLOGUE — *What the Dead Owe*
**Setting:** Baldur's Gate undercellars → the Cinderhaunt → a glimpse of the Fugue. Claustrophobic,
wet stone, candle-smoke and dread. **Length:** ~1 hour. *(Built ✅ as the playable slice.)*

- **Premise:** Tutorial by trauma. You die, see the Wall, are pulled back, and cut your way out.
- **Key beats:** the harvest; **your death**; the Fugue vision (the Wall, your reserved name, the
  unremembered word); waking Returned; the Cinderhaunt escape (first combat, first companion).
- **Set-piece:** *The Reservation.* A silent, playable walk along the Wall in the Fugue where you
  find the niche with your own name half-formed in it — before anything is explained.
- **Major development:** you are Returned; the world has a crack in it only you can fall through.
- **Twist seeded (not yet revealed):** the word spoken over your corpse. (It's your true name.)
- **Cliffhanger:** the Choir's herald flees in terror — *"They never come back"* — and points you,
  unwillingly, toward the man who runs them: Aldric Morn.

## ACT I — *The Gilded Gate*
**Setting:** Baldur's Gate — Upper City patriars, Lower City guilds, Outer City slums; Flaming Fist
patrols; the Guild in the shadows. Mood: a *Baldur's Gate* political thriller with a death-cult under it.

- **Premise:** Three powers (Aldric, Vayle, the Harpers) want you and the Crown's location. Build a
  party, work the city, reach the one place that knows where the Crown lies.
- **Key quests:**
  - *Tea with a Heretic* — your first, disarming meeting with **Aldric.** He's kind. He's right. It's
    awful.
  - *The Fist and the Knife* — navigate the Flaming Fist and the Guild; recruit **Roen.**
  - *A Devil's Arithmetic* — **Varra**'s introduction; a pact-collector in the Gate.
  - *The Cinderhaunt* (dungeon ✅) — clear the harvest-site; recover the first thread of the Crown's
    trail; obtain the key beneath the city.
  - *Ashes of Hollowmere* — Vayle burns a harvest-village to deny you the trail.
- **Set-piece:** *The Burning of Hollowmere.* You arrive as the Doomguides put a village to the torch
  "to stop the spread." You can save a handful, not all. Vayle meets your eyes across the fire and does
  not look away. Her "lesser evil," entire.
- **Major development:** the Choir is full of *true believers;* Aldric's case is not madness; Vayle's
  cruelty has a logic. The easy moral frame collapses.
- **Twist revealed:** at the climax you touch the Crown's psychic wake and **the world skips** — three
  heartbeats in a city in the sky, then back, nosebleeding, alone in having felt it. The crack opens.
- **Companion beats:** Garrow's quiet grief at her church's deeds; Roen stops reporting to the Harpers;
  Varra's pact tightens.
- **Cliffhanger:** the trail leads to **Candlekeep** — and the skips are getting longer.

## ACT II — *The Book and the Wall*
**Setting:** **Candlekeep** (the great library-fortress; lore-dense, hushed, *Planescape* register) →
the **Fugue Plane / City of Judgment** (grey eternity, the Wall, Kelemvor's silent court). Mood: from
scholarly mystery to cosmic vertigo.

- **Premise:** Find the truth of the Crown. Pay the price of entry to Candlekeep, then descend, alive,
  into the afterlife itself.
- **Key quests:**
  - *The Book It Doesn't Have* — secure entry to Candlekeep with a unique volume (a multi-path side
    epic in itself).
  - *The Sealed Stacks* — uncover the two truths: the Crown is **Myrkul**; **you are the crack.**
  - *The Long Walk* (Fugue) — descend to the City of Judgment; an audience with **Kelemvor.**
  - *The Niche With Your Name* — find, again, your reservation on the Wall — now further formed.
  - *Pull Her Out* — extract **Maerin** from the Wall. (Recruit; the stakes made flesh.)
  - Companion personal quests detonate (Garrow's mentor; Roen's purge; Varra's leash).
- **Set-piece:** *The Audience.* Kelemvor — weary, just, *bound* — tells you plainly that the Wall is
  not his law and not in his power to end, and that the thing growing beneath his city is older grief
  than even he can name. A god admits he is not in charge. The floor of the world feels thin.
- **Major development:** Maerin walks at your side — luminous, fading, *furious* — and refuses to be
  anyone's reason.
- **Twist revealed (the midpoint):** **the Unmade speaks.** The true antagonist is not a god or a relic
  but the condensed sorrow of every discarded soul — and its grievance is *just.* It offers the road
  back through time, and then it **tears** you out of your own century.
- **Cliffhanger:** you fall out of 1486 and land in a city in the sky, ten thousand years gone, on the
  worst day in the history of magic.

## ACT III — *The First Apocalypses*
**Setting:** **Netheril, −339 DR** (flying cities of impossible arcane splendor) → the **Crown Wars,
~−10,000 DR** (elven high courts and the first damnations). Mood: awe, grandeur, and the specific dread
of standing inside a catastrophe you know the ending of.

- **Premise:** The Unmade scatters you across the hinge-points it means to edit. Survive them, learn its
  nature, and reckon with the hunter who keeps arriving first.
- **Key quests (Netheril):**
  - *The Day the Sky Fell* — arrive at **Karsus's Folly;** witness a mortal try to become the god of
    magic; feel the Weave die; survive the **fall of a flying city.**
  - *The Seventh Enclave* — pull **Naeve** from the ruin; recruit the mother of the first apocalypse.
  - *Karsus's Last Theorem* — Naeve's quest; the math that could undo a world (and tempt the Unmade).
- **Key quests (Crown Wars):**
  - *The First Verdict* — witness the law of the Faithless enforced as a *choice;* recruit **Ilfaeril.**
  - *The Vote He Cast* — Ilfaeril's quest; confront the souls he damned.
  - *The Face Ahead* — track the figure who's been everywhere before you.
- **Set-pieces:** *The Falling City* (a desperate descent/escape as Netheril's enclave plummets, magic
  dead, gravity remembering); *The Court of Knives* (a Crown-War elven court where a single word damns a
  people, and you can change it — or learn you can't).
- **Major development:** you learn the Unmade's true nature — and that the Wall is a *decision,* witnessed
  by Ilfaeril, who voted for it.
- **Twist revealed:** the **Last Returned** lowers its hood and wears **your face, aged by certainty.** It
  remembers being you. It is not here to kill you; it is here to *spare you the long road.* And the Unmade
  makes its offer plain: *pull the first thread, and Maerin — and everyone — is saved.* Your "destiny" is
  revealed to be **déjà vu of another self's choices.**
- **Companion beats:** Naeve recognizes your soul's seam from a centuries-old proof in your own hand;
  Ilfaeril fears he's the Unmade's instrument; Maerin meets a version of the world where her death meant
  something and hates it.
- **Cliffhanger:** to understand the offer, you must see where the Crown was *made* — and where the Weave
  died a second time.

## ACT IV — *Where the Ink Runs*
**Setting:** the **Time of Troubles, 1358 DR** (gods walking as mortals; Waterdeep under a wrong sky) →
the **Spellplague, 1385 DR** (blue fire, merging worlds, optional causality). Mood: reality coming apart
at the seams; beauty and horror indistinguishable.

- **Premise:** Witness the making of the relic you carry and the murder that nearly let the Unmade win —
  and be shown, by your future self, exactly what victory costs.
- **Key quests:**
  - *The Skull and the Crown* — be present at **Myrkul's death** above Waterdeep and the forging of the
    **Crown of Horns;** the Crown-Voice drops its mask.
  - *The Second Murder* — the **Spellplague;** the Weave collapses; the timeline is a held breath.
  - *The Deathless Garden* — the Last Returned **shows you its world:** the timeline where you said yes —
    the Wall gone, the dead saved, Maerin alive, and *nothing ever ending or changing or meaning again.*
  - A companion's **point of no return** (a personal quest where they can die for good, or leave).
- **Set-piece:** *The Deathless Garden.* A playable walk through a paradise that is also a tomb — every
  person you love, safe, smiling, and *static,* repeating the same perfect moment forever. The most
  beautiful and most unbearable level in the game.
- **Major development:** the "good ending" the Unmade offers is **real and achievable** — and you now know
  precisely why it's damnation. No strawman remains.
- **Twist revealed:** what *kind* of fixed point you intend to be — the loop senses your choice forming.
- **Companion beats:** romances bloom or break; a companion may fall; Maerin's time runs visibly short.
- **Cliffhanger:** every thread — every age — converges on one room at the end of all things.

## ACT V — *The Crown of Horns*
**Setting:** the **Court of the Dead**, beneath the City of Judgment, with all eras bleeding together.
Mood: apocalypse as intimacy — the fate of all history decided in a single chamber, quietly.

- **Premise:** Convergence. Aldric (with the Crown), Myrkul (ready to shed him), the Unmade (vast beneath
  the floor), Vayle (the church's last army), Jergal (watching), and the Last Returned (closing the loop).
  Choose your alliances; seek Jergal's keyhole; answer the absence.
- **Key quests:**
  - *The Convergence* — broker, betray, or break each power; your Act I–IV flags decide who stands with you.
  - *The Keyhole* (golden path) — with Jergal, Naeve, Ilfaeril, and the **Mournlight**, assemble the means
    to *rewrite* the law rather than tear it down.
  - *The Name* (Nameless path) — recover your true name and learn you are the Unmade's **origin.**
  - *The Last Returned* — the reflection-duel; not to defeat a double, but to refuse to become it.
- **Set-piece:** *The Ledger.* In the final chamber, the Wall's entire ten-thousand-year ledger of the
  discarded is laid open, and — if you walk the golden road — you may judge them, **one soul at a time, by
  hand,** the tedious mercy Jergal abdicated. It can take as long as you let it. Each name is a story.
- **Major development & final twist:** the loop has **no beginning.** The first Faithless soul ever pulled
  back was **you;** the unremembered word is your own name; every Returned across history has been you,
  becoming the Last Returned, seeding the grief that pulls back the first you. *You are the author of your
  own sorrow, across all of time.*
- **The choice:** the one act the loop cannot predict, because you have never done it — to **break** it,
  to **complete** it, to **rewrite** it, to **seize** it, or to **walk away** from all of it. (See
  `04_ENDINGS.md`.)
- **Final image:** determined by your ending — a Wall coming down stone by stone; a paradise exhaling at
  last; a single soul choosing, for the first time in ten thousand years, to *stay gone* so the next might
  be free.

### Pacing & scope note
Ship **Prologue + Act I** first (the vertical slice, mostly built). Each later act is a self-contained
"era-region" (hub + 2–3 dungeons + companion beats) that can be built and tested in isolation, then
threaded onto the spine. The time-travel structure is a feature *and* a production strategy: each era is a
module.
