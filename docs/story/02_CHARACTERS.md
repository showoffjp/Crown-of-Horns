# 🎭 Characters — Backstories, Secrets, and Arcs

> Deep dossiers. Each entry: who they are, the wound they carry, the secret they're hiding, what
> they *want* vs. what they *need*, their arc, and (for companions) personal quest, approval
> triggers, romance, and possible fates. Spoiler-complete.

---

# THE PROTAGONIST — *the Returned*

**Role:** Player-created (any race/class/background). **Fixed truth:** you die in the prologue and
are pulled back. You are *Returned* — you perceive the lingering dead, and a seam runs down the
middle of your soul that is, in truth, a seam in time.

**The universal backstory (true regardless of build):** You were, once, ordinary — or believed you
were. The seam was always there; the harvest only widened it. What no version of you remembers is
that this is not your first death, or your hundredth. You are the **First Discarded** — the first
soul the law of the Faithless ever damned, ten thousand years ago, pulled back by a grief that did
not yet have a name. Every life you have lived since has ended the same way: death, the Wall, the
hand that pulls you back, the long road to a throne, and a new grief seeded into the absence that
will pull back the *next* you. You are a closed loop wearing a different face each cycle. The game
is the one cycle in which you might learn this in time to *choose* differently.

**The four backgrounds are four facets of the same wound:**
- **Candlekeep Acolyte** — As a child you read a chronicle that should not exist and saw, for a
  heartbeat, your own true name. You forgot it on purpose; recovering it is the lore route.
- **Flaming Fist Deserter** — You died once already in a war you fled; the guilt is the seam's
  cover story. Contacts and a debt in the Gate.
- **Doomguide's Ward** — You served Kelemvor's church *under Aldric Morn.* You loved him like a
  father. The most intimate, most painful road to the central villain.
- **The Nameless** *(the true route)* — You woke after your death with **no past at all.** The Wall
  remembers you even though you don't. Your forgotten name is the master key to the whole mystery,
  and the whole game is the slow, dreadful recovery of *who you were the first time you died* —
  which is to say, the recovery of the Unmade's name, which is to say, your own.

**Want vs. need:** You *want* to live — to keep the second life that was stolen back for you. You
*need* to understand that the thing keeping pulling you back is your own unfinished grief, and that
the only act of love left to you may be to finally, truly, *stay gone.*

# THE ANTAGONISTS — *four masks over one face*

## MASK I — ALDRIC MORN, the Doomguide Who Said No
**Role:** The Act I villain; a fallen paladin/cleric of Kelemvor. The charismatic, sympathetic
mentor-as-monster.

**Backstory:** Born to a plague-cart family who survived by burying others, Aldric took the grey of
the **Doomguides** — Kelemvor's clergy, who lay the restless dead, hunt the undead, and *believe in
the order of death* as a mercy. For forty years he was the best of them on the Sword Coast: he
buried plague-villages with his own hands, comforted the dying, and never once doubted that death,
properly kept, is kind. He married late, to a woman named **Saerin**, a cartographer who believed in
maps and weather and nothing else. Their daughter **Maerin** inherited her mother's cheerful
faithlessness — she loved the world too much to need a god behind it.

Then the **Howling Pox** took Saerin and Maerin both in one winter. Aldric, who had given a thousand
strangers their last rites, performed his own family's — and then, half-mad with grief and certain
of his god's mercy, he used a forbidden Doomguide rite to *follow* them into the Fugue, to see them
judged kindly. Saerin, who had loved him, was claimed by no god and sorted to the Wall. Maerin, who
believed in nothing, was mortared in beside her. Aldric stood before **Kelemvor** himself and begged.
And the god of the dead — not cruel, but *bound* by a law older than his throne — told him the truth:
the Wall is not Kelemvor's law. No god made it. It simply *is,* and not even death can countermand it.

Aldric came back wrong. He did not rage. He did *arithmetic.* If no god would tear down the Wall, he
would become the god who could. He found the Choir — others broken the same way — and the lure that
would let a mortal seize divinity: the **Crown of Horns.** He has counted every life his harvest will
cost and he will answer for each one, gladly, because against an *eternity* of the Faithless dissolving
into nothing, a few thousand deaths is the smallest price ever asked for the largest mercy.

**Secret:** He does not know the Crown is Myrkul, riding him. He does not know that the "vision" that
sent him into the Fugue was *seeded* — by the Unmade, by the Last Returned, by you, in a prior cycle.
He is a good man, weaponized by his own grief, by a parasite god, and by the loop you are.

**Want vs. need:** *Wants* to tear down the Wall and save the Faithless (including his family). *Needs*
to understand that the cure he's building is a tyranny worse than the disease — and that Maerin,
pulled out of the Wall, does not *want* to be his reason. The most devastating scene in the game is
the one where you can let his own resurrected daughter tell him so.

**Possible fates:** Killed; redeemed (talked down, if Maerin lives and you've earned it); ascended
(you *help* him — and watch Myrkul shed him like a husk the instant he wins); or spared to live with
what he's done.

## MASK II — MYRKUL, the Crown-Voice
**Role:** The dead god in the relic; a secret "companion" (the Pale Companion) from Act II; the
mid-game true-power reveal.

**Backstory:** One of the **Dead Three** — Bane, Bhaal, Myrkul — mortals who clawed their way to
godhood ages ago. Myrkul was the **Lord of the Dead** before Cyric, before Kelemvor; a miser of souls,
patient as stone. He *died* in the Time of Troubles (1358 DR) above Waterdeep, and his skull was forged
into the **Crown of Horns** — a relic that holds the last sediment of his divinity. He has spent over a
century in that relic, learning the one trick that can return a dead god to the throne: not force, but
**a sympathetic mortal who will do the climbing for him.** Aldric is the best vehicle he has ever found
— a grieving saint the whole world will *root for.* Myrkul intends to let Aldric ignite the Crown, walk
it into the City of Judgment, and then **shed him** and take the throne himself.

**Secret:** Myrkul fears exactly one thing, and it is not you and not Kelemvor: it is the **Unmade.**
Because the Unmade is made of the souls *his* Wall dissolved, and if it ever unwrites the law, Myrkul's
entire claim to the dead unravels with it. Myrkul is, hilariously and horribly, on *your* side against
the Unmade — and will betray you the instant that danger passes.

## MASK III — THE UNMADE, the Absence That Grieves
**Role:** The cosmic true antagonist; revealed Act II; understood across Act III; *answered* in Act V.
Also called the **Hollow Choir**, the **Sum of Nothing**, the **Tithe Unpaid.**

**Backstory:** When the law of the Faithless dissolves a soul, the cosmos insists it becomes *nothing.*
But nothing is the one substance creation cannot actually produce — every "nothing" is a *something*
unaccounted for. Ten thousand years of dissolved Faithless souls did not vanish. They **pooled**, below
the Fugue Plane, in the place the gods refuse to name, and slowly — the way a beach becomes stone — they
**accreted into a single grieving awareness.** It speaks with every voice it was made of, all at once. It
is not evil. It is the **condensed sorrow of everyone the cosmos threw away,** and it has reached a single
lucid conclusion: the arithmetic that made it should *never have been written.* The only edit large enough
is to walk time back to the source and unmake the law of the Faithless — which would, incidentally,
unwrite everything built on top of it: all of history since. It does not consider that a flaw. *Better
nothing than this,* it reasons, *and we would know.*

**Secret (the keystone):** The Unmade did not begin when the first soul dissolved. It is a **closed
causal loop** — it pulled back its **first** soul (you, the First Discarded) at the very founding of the
law, *before* it had accreted enough to be aware, using grief it would only later generate. There is no
first cause. The Unmade *is* the loop, and the loop *is* you, grieving yourself across all of time. To
"defeat" it is incoherent; its grievance is just and its existence is yours. You can only *answer* it.

## MASK IV — THE LAST RETURNED, the You That Already Lost
**Role:** The recurring hunter (Act III on); the reflection-boss; the existential climax. Also called
the **Pale Sovereign**, the **One Who Said Yes.**

**Backstory:** In the cycle *before* this one — or the thousand cycles before — a version of you reached
the Court of the Dead, was offered the Unmade's bargain, and **said yes.** It pulled the thread. It
abolished death. And it became the Unmade's avatar and the steward of a deathless, changeless, perfect,
*frozen* world — and then, slowly, across centuries of a paradise where nothing ends and so nothing means,
it understood what it had done and could not undo it. So it does the only thing left: it walks back through
the loop to find its *earlier* self — you — and tries to make you choose its road *faster,* to "spare" you
the long centuries of regret it endured. It is not lying when it says it remembers being you. It is not
wrong that, on the road you're walking, you become it. It hunts you out of a love so corroded it looks
exactly like cruelty.

**Want vs. need:** *Wants* you to complete the loop, because a closed loop is the only thing that has ever
felt like peace to it. *Needs* — though it can no longer want it — for the loop to finally *break,* even at
the cost of its own existence. The defector-fragment (a secret late companion) is the sliver of it that
still needs that.

## THE INSTITUTIONAL FOIL — SISTER VAYLE, Defender of the Wall
**Role:** Acts I–II antagonist; a true believer, not a villain; a possible late ally you'll despise.

**Backstory:** Vayle rose through Kelemvor's church not from cruelty but from **terror disciplined into
duty.** As a novice she read the sealed archive — the **Ledger of the Unwalled** — that records every
prior attempt to reform the afterlife. Each one loosed something worse: a Spellplague, a god-war, a
city of the screaming undying. She concluded, coldly, that the Wall is an atrocity that must be *kept,*
because the alternatives are catastrophes. She would rather guard hell forever than gamble heaven. She
hunts Aldric, detains you, burns the harvest-village — and every cruelty is, in her own mind, a smaller
number than the cruelty she's preventing. She is what Aldric would call you if you choose the status-quo
ending: someone who looked at the Wall, understood it was monstrous, and *defended it anyway.*

**Secret:** She is dying (a wasting illness) and has not told her order — and she is **Faithless herself,**
having lost her god decades ago and kept up the rite for the church's sake. She knows the Wall is waiting
for her, personally, and she defends it anyway. That is either the bravest or the most broken thing in the
game, and you get to decide which.

## THE NEUTRAL DOOR — JERGAL, the Bored Scribe
**Role:** The eldest dead-god; not a faction; the keyhole to the golden ending.

**Backstory:** Before Myrkul, before the Dead Three, **Jergal** was the Lord of the End of Everything —
and he found eternity so *tedious* that he voluntarily handed his godhood to three ambitious mortals and
took a clerk's job, recording the dead with the patience of a man who has read the last page and found it
unsurprising. He has watched the Unmade grow for ten thousand years and said nothing, because he is
*curious* — the only feeling he has left. He will not help you and will not stop you. But he holds the one
piece of esoteric truth that makes the **golden ending** possible: that the law of the Faithless can be
*rewritten* rather than torn down, if someone is willing to do the unbearably tedious work of judging every
discarded soul *by hand,* one at a time, forever. He'd find that hilarious. He half-hopes you'll try.

# THE COMPANIONS

> Seven recruitable, across three eras, plus the secret two. Each can be argued with, lost, and (some)
> loved. Approval lives in `companion.<id>.approval`.

## SISTER GARROW — the Doomguide who stayed *(Cleric · present)*
- **Backstory:** Trained beside Aldric; idolized him. When he fell, she *didn't* follow — and has hated
  herself for the cowardice of obedience ever since. She now tends the Unbound at the city's edges, laying
  the dead her own church's harvest unmade.
- **Secret:** She privately agrees the Wall is monstrous. She stays Kelemvor's servant because she believes
  Aldric's cure damns *more* souls than the Wall ever has — and she is terrified that belief is just fear
  wearing the mask of faith.
- **Wound / want / need:** *Wound:* obedience that felt like betrayal. *Wants* to be certain again. *Needs*
  to learn that faith without doubt is just obedience, and to choose her own creed at last.
- **Personal quest — "The Office of the Dead":** her old mentor has risen Unbound; she must lay him, and
  decide whether to do it by the rite or by mercy.
- **Approval:** honoring the dead, mercy, hard moral courage. **Loses:** desecration, cruelty, glib nihilism.
- **Romance:** yes — slow, grave, tender; a romance that is really a long argument about whether anything
  is sacred. **Fates:** can become the new High Doomguide (reformist), die laying her mentor, or break with
  the church entirely to follow your road.

## ROEN ALLEYWIND — the Harper who stopped reporting *(Bard/Rogue · present)*
- **Backstory:** Orphan of the Gate's lower wards, recruited by the **Harpers** for a quick tongue and
  quicker fingers. He's run a hundred ops and never once let one touch him. You're the first.
- **Secret:** His handlers ordered him to *use and discard you* — a soul with a hole in it is a liability the
  Harpers won't abide. He stopped filing reports three weeks ago. He's choosing you over the only family he's
  ever had, and it terrifies him, so he jokes.
- **Wound / want / need:** *Wound:* belonging only ever came with a price tag. *Wants* to keep everyone at
  arm's length and alive. *Needs* to let one person all the way in and survive it.
- **Personal quest — "The Song That Names You":** a Harper purge forces him to choose the network or the
  party; you can save both, neither, or him.
- **Approval:** wit, loyalty under fire, protecting the small. **Loses:** sanctimony, betrayal, cruelty to
  the weak. **Romance:** yes — banter that curdles into the realest thing either of you has had.
  **Fates:** Harper spymaster, exile, or the one who holds your hand at the end.

## VARRA — the tiefling running from her own pact *(Warlock · present)*
- **Backstory:** Sold into a pact as a child by a desperate parent; she's spent her life one missed payment
  from collection, hiding behind a wall of gallows wit.
- **Secret:** Her patron is a death-power *circling the Crown of Horns* — possibly Myrkul's own agent. Her
  pact may be a **leash** the villains can yank at the worst moment, and she knows it.
- **Wound / want / need:** *Wound:* she was *currency* before she was a person. *Wants* freedom from the
  pact. *Needs* to learn she's worth more than what she was traded for.
- **Personal quest — "The Fine Print":** renegotiate or shatter her pact, at a cost that tests what she'll
  pay for freedom. **Approval:** audacity, loyalty, gallows humor, defying authority. **Loses:** self-
  righteousness, leaving people behind. **Fates:** free and unmade-of-fear, bound forever, or sacrificed.

## NAEVE OF THE SEVENTH ENCLAVE — the mother of the first apocalypse *(Wizard · Netheril, −339)*
- **Backstory:** A Netherese arcanist of the flying cities, brilliant and proud, who helped derive the
  theorem that **Karsus** weaponized into his Folly. You pull her from the rubble the instant the Weave dies
  and her city falls from the sky. She has lost *everything* — her world, her people, her certainty that
  knowledge is clean.
- **Secret:** She has read the forbidden marginalia and suspects the Unmade is real — and she has seen the
  seam in your soul *before,* in a proof written in a hand identical to yours, dated centuries before your
  birth. She is the first to suspect you are a loop.
- **Wound / want / need:** *Wound:* her genius *ended a world.* *Wants* to undo Karsus's Folly (and is thus
  dangerously sympathetic to the Unmade's "just undo the mistake" logic). *Needs* to accept that some
  mistakes can only be *carried,* not erased.
- **Personal quest — "Karsus's Last Theorem":** recover the rest of the proof and choose whether to *use* it.
- **Approval:** curiosity, intellectual honesty, restraint with power. **Loses:** willful ignorance, reckless
  ambition. **Romance:** yes — two people who've each ended a world, learning to be carried. **Fates:** she
  can be the one who talks the Unmade *down,* or the one who hands it the theorem to win.

## ILFAERIL — the elf who voted for the Wall *(Paladin/Ranger · Crown Wars, ~−10,000)*
- **Backstory:** A high elf lord of the Crown Wars who, in a council of vengeance, **voted to damn** an
  enemy people's souls — one of the verdicts that hardened into the cosmic law of the Faithless. He has
  spent ten thousand years as a half-ghost at the Fugue's edge, atoning by guiding lost souls past the Wall.
- **Secret:** He is living proof of the game's most dangerous truth — that the Wall is a *decision,* not
  physics. The Unmade reveres him as its first witness. He fears that makes him its instrument.
- **Wound / want / need:** *Wound:* a single vote, ten thousand years of consequence. *Wants* to atone.
  *Needs* to learn that atonement is not the same as self-erasure — that he is allowed to *stop* paying.
- **Personal quest — "The Verdict":** confront the descendants (souls) of those he damned and choose between
  their forgiveness and their vengeance. **Approval:** mercy, oaths kept, humility. **Loses:** cruelty,
  pride, despair. **Fates:** finally at peace, or martyred holding the Wall open so souls can flee.

## MAERIN — the girl from the Wall *(Ranger / undead-touched · the stakes, embodied)*
- **Backstory:** Aldric's daughter; cheerful, godless, beloved; dead of the Howling Pox at sixteen; mortared
  into the Wall beside her mother. You can pull her *out* in Act II. She comes back luminous, fading, furious,
  and entirely herself.
- **Secret:** She is half-dissolved and *running out of time* even outside the Wall — and she has her own,
  fierce opinion that the world should **not** burn to save her. She refuses to be her father's reason, or
  yours.
- **Wound / want / need:** *Wound:* she died for *nothing,* by the cosmos's own accounting. *Wants* to be a
  *person,* not a symbol or a stake. *Needs* — and this is the tragedy — perhaps to be allowed to finish dying
  on her own terms.
- **Personal quest — "Not Your Reason":** the most direct line to Aldric's heart; her choices can break him,
  redeem him, or harden him into Myrkul's perfect vessel. **Approval:** honesty, treating her as capable,
  letting her *choose.* **Loses:** condescension, using her, sentimentality. **Romance:** *no* (deliberately;
  she is the game's "some loves are not that kind" — a fierce, platonic heart). **Fates:** she can be saved (at
  terrible cost), choose to return to the Wall to free others, or simply, finally, *rest* — and which of those
  you can live with is the whole game in miniature.

## (SECRET) THE PALE COMPANION & THE MOURNLIGHT
- **The Pale Companion** is the **Crown-Voice** — Myrkul — riding in your pack from Act II, offering reasonable,
  monstrous counsel; a Planescape-style philosopher who is also a final enemy. Heeding it grants power and lies
  in equal measure.
- **The Mournlight** is the secret late companion: a **defector fragment of the Last Returned** — the sliver of
  your future self that still, against everything, wants the loop to *break.* It is your own grief, finally on
  your side. Recruiting it (lore-gated, hard) is the key to the golden and the tragic endings — the proof that
  even the thing you become can choose, at the last, to want an end.
