# 💞 Romance Scripts — The Slow Burns

> Full romance arcs for the four romanceable companions. Each romance is **an argument about one of
> the game's themes** — Garrow/faith, Roen/belonging, Naeve/guilt, Varra/worth — so that falling in
> love is also the player *taking a position* on what the game is about. Structured in six stages,
> gated by approval (`appr.<id>`) and act progression, with key-beat dialogue. Spoiler-complete; ties
> into the Breach (`15`), the Mirror (`10` §3), and the endings.

> **The rule that makes them hurt:** love here is never a *reward.* It's a *stake.* Every romance
> raises what you stand to lose — to the Wall, to the loop, to the ending you choose. The happiest
> romance and the most devastating ending can be the same playthrough.

> **The six stages** (shared spine): **Spark** (Act I) → **Trust** (Act II) → **Turn** (the first
> true thing, Act II/III) → **Crisis** (their personal quest / a loss, Act III/IV) → **Choosing**
> (commitment or rupture, Act IV) → **The Last Night** (Act V, before the Court of the Dead).

---

## 1. SISTER GARROW — *a romance about whether anything is sacred*

> Theme: **faith vs. doubt.** Loving Garrow is the argument that something can be holy even in a
> cosmos this cruel. The tragedy: she has spent her life refusing to believe she'll see anyone again,
> and you are the first thing that makes her *want* to be wrong.

**Spark (Act I).** No flirtation. It begins with *honesty* — the first time she tells you a doubt she
has never said aloud, in the dark, laying an Unbound child to rest.
> **Garrow:** "I performed that rite ten thousand times before I let myself wonder if it sends them
> somewhere worse. You don't say that, in the grey. You just dig." *(a long pause)* "...I don't know
> why I'm telling you. You're the one person who's actually *been* there. Tell me I'm wrong to wonder."

**Trust (Act II).** She starts seeking you out before hard duties — not for help, for *witness.* The
beat: she asks you to stand with her while she does something her church forbids (a small mercy for a
godless corpse), and trusts you not to report it.

**The Turn (Act II/III).** On a battlefield of the dead, the line that breaks her open:
> **Garrow:** "I have buried a thousand people and never once let myself believe I'd see any of them
> again. It was the only way to keep digging." *(she looks at you)* "You walked *out* of the place
> I send them. You're the first thing in my whole life that's made me want to be wrong about all of
> it. And I don't have a rite for that. I don't have a *prayer* for that. I just have — you. Standing
> there. Being impossible." *(fx: SET romance.garrow.turn)*

**Crisis.** Her quest *"The Office of the Dead"* — laying her Unbound mentor. If you've romanced her,
the choice she makes is *for you as much as for her faith*: she can lay him by the rite (and you hold
her while she weeps), or commit heresy and let him wander (and you become the only one who knows). The
romance deepens either way, but in different keys — grief-shared, or secret-shared.

**Choosing (Act IV).** After the Breach, after a loss, she comes to you with the most un-Garrow thing
she owns: a *hope.*
> **Garrow:** "I keep a list. Everyone I've buried. I add to it every night; it's the closest thing I
> have to a prayer. Tonight I — " *(she can't quite)* " — tonight I wrote your name at the top of a
> *different* list. The one of people I refuse to bury. I don't know how to keep a list like that. I've
> never had one. Teach me. Stay alive long enough to teach me."

**The Last Night (Act V).** Before the Court of the Dead. Quiet. She does not ask you to survive — she
knows what's coming. She asks for *now.*
> **Garrow:** "I'm not going to pretend tomorrow isn't what it is. I've sat with too many people on
> their last night to lie on ours. So: no promises. No someday. Just — here. You. Me. The fire. And me
> finally, *finally* believing, for one night, that the dead might not be gone forever. You gave me
> that. Whatever happens at that Court — you already gave me that." *(fx: SET romance.garrow.consummated)*

**Integration with the endings.** Garrow's romance reaches its devastating peak in **Break the Loop**:
she is the one who gives you the last rite — *your own* — as you step into the niche, weeping, speaking
your true name so it is never again forgotten. *"I have given ten thousand people their rites and meant
none of them like this. Go easy. I'll keep your name. I'll keep it forever. I promise — and I've never
once meant a promise about the dead, until you."*

## 2. ROEN ALLEYWIND — *a romance about letting someone in and surviving it*

> Theme: **belonging.** Roen's whole life trained him that attachment is a liability. The romance is
> him learning that the most naked thing a person can do is *stay.* Banter that curdles, gloriously,
> into the realest thing he's ever had.

**Spark (Act I).** All performance. He flirts the way he picks locks — fast, skilled, to get past your
defenses, never his own. Every line has an exit built in.
> **Roen:** "I flirt with everyone, it's a professional reflex, don't read into it." *(beat)* "...You're
> reading into it. Stop that. I can see you reading into it. Gods, you're as bad as me."

**Trust (Act II).** It cracks when he stops reporting to the Harpers *for you* — and can't make a joke
about that one. The first time the performance fails:
> **Roen:** "I had three reports due on you. I wrote none of them. Do you understand what that — no, you
> don't, you *can't*, the Harpers are the only — " *(he stops, genuinely thrown)* "Why am I telling you
> the truth? I don't *do* the truth. You're a menace. This is a menace situation."

**The Turn (Act III).** After the Purge (his quest), exhausted, stripped of his deflections:
> **Roen:** "I've conned everyone I've ever met into thinking I'm fine. My handlers, the Guild, the
> *mirror.* You're the first person I can't lie to, and it is the most naked I have ever felt with my
> clothes *on*, and I think — I think that's what people mean when they say love, and I hate it, and I
> have never wanted anything to stop less in my entire life. Don't you dare stop." *(fx: SET
> romance.roen.turn)*

**Crisis.** *"The Song That Names You."* If romanced, the chapter where he tells you the name he was
*born* with — the orphan name he buried under "Roen Alleywind" — happens in the same arc as your own
search for *your* true name. Two people who threw their names away, digging each other's back up. It's
the most intimate thing he's ever done, and he does it terrified.

**Choosing (Act IV).** He offers the one thing he's never offered anyone: *a future tense.*
> **Roen:** "Here's the thing about me you should know before you decide anything. I don't do 'after.'
> There's never been an after. There's the job, and the next job, and the grave. But lately I keep —
> *catching* myself. Imagining an after. An inn. Two chairs. You, complaining about the wine. And it
> *terrifies* me, because it means I've got something to lose now, and I have spent my whole life making
> sure I never did. So. Yeah. Congratulations. You ruined a perfectly good survival strategy. I'm yours."

**The Last Night (Act V).** He doesn't do solemn. So he does the bravest thing he knows — he *plans the
after out loud*, knowing it might not come, because saying it is the most honest he can be:
> **Roen:** "After this, we're getting that inn. The Wandering Niche, I'll call it, it's a *terrible*
> name, you'll hate it. Two chairs. No missions. I'll teach you to cheat at cards; you'll be bad at it;
> it'll be the best years of both our lives." *(quieter)* "I know. I *know* what tomorrow is. I'm saying
> it anyway. Out loud. With my whole chest. Because if I don't get the after — at least I got to *want*
> one. You gave me that. Now shut up and let me pretend with you, just for tonight." *(fx: SET
> romance.roen.consummated)*

**Integration with the endings.** In **Break the Loop**, Roen is the one who *holds your hand* as you
step into the niche — and for once he has no joke, just: *"...Okay. Okay. I had a whole bit ready and I
can't — I can't do the bit. Just — I found you. In all of it, all the cycles, I found you once. That
has to be enough. Go on. I've got you. I've got you."*

## 3. NAEVE OF THE SEVENTH ENCLAVE — *a romance about whether you're allowed to be loved after what you did*

> Theme: **guilt.** Two people who each ended a world, circling the question of whether they're
> permitted to be happy anyway. A romance of *arguments* — long, brilliant, late-night debates about
> determinism and culpability that slowly stop being philosophy and start being *them.*

**Spark (Act III).** Intellectual sparring, instant and total. She finds you interesting before she
finds you attractive, which for Naeve is the deeper compliment.
> **Naeve:** "You are a logical impossibility, you realize. A soul that predates itself. I should find
> you horrifying. Instead I find you — " *(she frowns, displeased with herself)* " — *interesting.* Do
> not let it go to your head. I once found a paradox interesting and it ended a civilization."

**Trust (Act II/III).** She shows you the proof — Karsus's theorem, the thing she helped build, the
mathematics that fell a sky. Letting you see it is letting you see her crime.
> **Naeve:** "Everyone I have ever told has either recoiled or absolved me. I want neither. I want
> someone to look at the worst thing I ever did, understand it completely, and *neither* run *nor*
> forgive. Just — stay in the room with it. With me. Can you do that? Most cannot."

**The Turn (Act IV).** After she finds your handwriting in the margin of the proof — after she *proves*
you're a loop — the argument finally turns personal:
> **Naeve:** "I have spent a thousand years certain the universe is a proof and we are all merely steps
> in it. Determinism is a *comfort*, you understand — if every step is forced, then my step, the one
> that ended the world, was not truly mine. And then *you* — you did the one thing my mathematics cannot
> model. You *chose.* You broke the proof by existing. And if you can choose — " *(her voice does
> something it has not done in a thousand years)* " — then so did I. Which means it *was* mine. The
> sky. All of it. Mine." *(a long silence)* "I have never been able to grieve it, because I never let it
> be mine to grieve. You gave me back my guilt. That is the most appalling gift anyone has ever given
> me, and I find that I love you for it, which is also impossible, and I am *done* arguing with what's
> impossible where you're concerned." *(fx: SET romance.naeve.turn)*

**Crisis.** *"Karsus's Last Theorem."* If romanced, she burns the theorem *for you* — chooses to let
her life's masterwork die rather than risk it becoming the Unmade's tool — and it is the first time she
has ever valued a person over a proof.

**Choosing (Act IV).** No grand speech — Naeve doesn't have those. She does something better: she
*revises her axioms.*
> **Naeve:** "I have built my entire understanding of existence on the premise that meaning is an
> illusion the living tell themselves. I would like to formally amend it. Premise, revised: *you* are
> meaning. Not in the abstract. Specifically. Demonstrably. I have checked the work. It holds. I am, it
> turns out, in love, and I have the proof, and for once in my life I am *glad* to be wrong about the
> universe being empty."

**The Last Night (Act V).** She wants to *understand* the ending with you, because understanding is how
she loves:
> **Naeve:** "I cannot promise you tomorrow; I have run the equations and tomorrow is genuinely
> indeterminate, which — for me — is the most romantic thing I can offer you. An *open* variable. A
> future I cannot solve. I have hated open variables my entire existence." *(she takes your hand,
> studies it like a proof)* "Tonight I love them. Tonight I love everything I cannot solve, because all
> of it is *you.* Stay. Let us be unsolved together, for one night, before the world makes us answer."
> *(fx: SET romance.naeve.consummated)*

**Integration with the endings.** Naeve is essential to **Jergal's Keyhole** — and if romanced, the
golden ending's final image is the two of you at the Ledger, *deriving the rewritten Law together*, an
eternity of judging the dead by hand turned into the longest, gentlest collaboration in the multiverse:
*"An infinite proof. One soul at a time. I can think of no better way to spend forever, and no one I
would rather spend it not-solving with."*

## 4. VARRA — *a romance about being worth more than your price* (compact)

> Theme: **worth.** A war of attrition against her own certainty that she's unlovable because she's
> *priced.* She tests you cruelly, assumes the worst, pushes you away — and the romance is won not by
> grand gestures but by *staying through the tests.*

- **Spark:** pure deflective acid. Every flirt is a dare you'll prove her right by leaving.
- **The Turn:** *"You know the worst thing about being sold? Some part of you agrees you were worth
  selling. I have spent a year refusing to agree, and I have no idea what to do with that except —
  apparently — this. Don't make me regret refusing."*
- **Crisis:** *"The Fine Print."* If you helped her void the pact (rather than letting the Breach take
  her — see `15`), the romance survives into the late game; the leash is gone, and so is the receipt.
- **Finale:** if freed and romanced, her ending is the two of you **burning the receipt** at last —
  the literal contract for her soul, ash on the wind, her worth finally un-priceable.

> **Note on the Breach:** Varra's romance carries the game's sharpest stakes — if you *don't* complete
> her pact-quest in time, she is the companion the Wall takes in `15_SCENE_THE_BREACH.md`. A romanced
> Varra lost to the Breach is one of the game's most devastating possible playthroughs: the receipt
> collected before you ever got to burn it.

## Design Notes

- **Approval gates, not just flag flips:** each stage requires both act-progression *and* an approval
  threshold, so romance is *earned* by aligning with who the companion is — Garrow rewards mercy, Roen
  rewards loyalty-under-fire, Naeve rewards intellectual honesty, Varra rewards staying.
- **Mutual exclusivity, lightly held:** pursuing two romances past the **Turn** triggers a brief,
  in-character reckoning (no melodrama — these are adults), forcing a choice before **Choosing.**
- **Romance ↔ stakes:** a romanced companion is the most likely **`anchor`** for the finale (see `13`
  §V), so the cosmos lands on the face you chose to love. The game's cruelty and its tenderness use the
  *same* lever — which is the whole point.
