# 🌅 The Endings

> Six endings (plus a hidden seventh failure-state), diverging on what you believe a **soul**, an
> **ending**, and a **self** are worth. Each lists its in-world meaning, how it's reached (the
> `GameFlags` that gate it), its final image, and the spread of **companion / faction epilogue
> slides** (BG2-style). No ending is "the win." Each is a cost you choose to bear.

---

## How endings are determined
The Court of the Dead reads your accumulated state — there is no single "ending button." The branch
opens based on flags set across all five acts:

- **`unmade.understood`** — did you grasp the Unmade's nature (vs. only fighting it)?
- **`pc.true_name`** — did you recover your name (the Nameless/lore route)?
- **`companion.mournlight.recruited`** — did the defector-fragment of your future self join you?
- **`jergal.keyhole`** — did you assemble the means to *rewrite* the law (Jergal + Naeve + Ilfaeril)?
- **`maerin.fate`** (saved / rested / returned) and **`aldric.fate`** (killed / redeemed / ascended).
- Faction reputations (`faction.kelemvor`, `faction.choir`, `faction.harpers`, `faction.unbroken`).
- Romance flags; companion survival flags.

> The two "best" endings (Break the Loop, Jergal's Keyhole) are **hard to reach** — they require
> understanding rather than victory. The most *seductive* ending (Abolition) is the easiest, and the
> most damning. This is deliberate.

---

## 1. 🜍 ABOLITION-BY-ATROCITY — *"The Mercy That Ate the World"*
**Choice:** Say *yes* to the Unmade. Pull the first thread.
**Requires:** side with the Choir / Unmade; `aldric.fate = ascended` or you take the thread yourself.

You help the Unmade unwrite the law of the Faithless at its source. Across every age at once, the Wall
unravels; every discarded soul — Saerin, the Crown-War damned, the Netherese dead, *Maerin* — is saved,
restored, *freed.* And the price comes due exactly as the Last Returned promised: death itself unbinds.
The living die in oceans to feed the edit; time knots into a single deathless, changeless Now. You take
your place as the Unmade's avatar on a throne of saved souls, in a perfect world that will never change
again — and the Last Returned smiles, because now there is no Last Returned. **There is only you, and
you have always been here, and you always will be.** The loop closes, seamless and eternal and kind and
dead.
- **Final image:** the Deathless Garden, blooming forever, every face you loved smiling the same smile,
  forever, while you sit at its center and slowly forget what *change* was for.
- **Tone:** the horror ending dressed as the happy one. You got everything you wanted. *That's the tragedy.*

## 2. ⚖️ THE DOOMGUIDE'S PEACE — *"The Atrocity Kept"*
**Choice:** Side with Vayle/Kelemvor. Stop Aldric. Defend the Wall.
**Requires:** high `faction.kelemvor`; defeat Aldric and the Unmade by force; `jergal.keyhole = false`.

You decide the alternatives are worse, and you keep the Wall. Aldric falls; Myrkul is denied; the Unmade
is beaten back into silence (not answered — *suppressed*). Death's order endures. Vayle, dying, names you
her successor or her executioner. And you live — or die — knowing you looked at the oldest cruelty in the
multiverse, understood it completely, and **defended it anyway,** because the cost of mercy was too high.
You are what Aldric warned you you'd become.
- **Final image:** the Wall, intact, a single new niche being filled — and you turning away from it,
  because you cannot bear to watch and cannot bring yourself to stop it.
- **Tone:** cold triumph; the bitterness of being *responsible.*

## 3. 🗝️ JERGAL'S KEYHOLE — *"The Tedious Mercy"* (the golden ending)
**Choice:** Neither tear the Wall down nor keep it — **rewrite** it, by hand, forever.
**Requires:** `jergal.keyhole = true` (Jergal's counsel + Naeve's theorem + Ilfaeril's witness);
`unmade.understood = true`; the Mournlight recruited.

The hardest road. You realize the Unmade's grievance can be *answered* instead of obeyed: the law of the
Faithless can be **rewritten** rather than abolished — every discarded soul judged honestly, one at a
time, by a hand willing to do the unbearably tedious work forever. Jergal, delighted, hands you the pen
he laid down ages ago. The Unmade, *seen* at last — its dead acknowledged, its arithmetic corrected —
chooses peace over unmaking. The Wall comes down stone by stone, soul by soul, *judged,* not dissolved.
It will take you the rest of eternity. You take the job. Heaven exhales for the first time in ten thousand
years.
- **Final image:** you at a vast desk at the end of the world, the Ledger open, calling the first
  discarded soul by its true name — and it is *yours,* and you forgive it, and the loop, for the first
  time, has somewhere to *end.*
- **Tone:** earned, melancholy hope. The triumph that costs you eternity and is worth it.

## 4. 👑 THE RETURNED THRONE — *"The New Tyrant, Maybe Merciful"*
**Choice:** Take the Crown of Horns yourself.
**Requires:** claim the Crown; outcome branches on your accumulated mercy/cruelty flags.

You seize the throne of the dead — and become exactly the thing you've spent the game watching others
become. The epilogue forks hard on who you've *been:* a merciful new judge who reforms the afterlife from
the throne (a lesser, lonelier Keyhole), or the seed of the next catastrophe a future Returned will have
to walk back and stop. Either way, you are now a fixed point. Either way, the loop has a new face — yours.
- **Final image:** you, crowned, on Kelemvor's empty throne, the dead lined up before you — and a faint,
  familiar nosebleed as the *next* crack begins to open somewhere in time.
- **Tone:** ambition's reward; the snake eating its tail with a crown on.

## 5. 🕯️ BREAK THE LOOP — *"Stay Gone"* (the existential ending)
**Choice:** Refuse the Unmade *and* refuse to become the Last Returned — by accepting your own, final,
true death.
**Requires:** `pc.true_name = true`; the Mournlight recruited; refuse all thrones and bargains.

The only act in ten thousand years the loop cannot predict, because you have never done it: you *stay
gone.* You understand at last that the Unmade is your own ungrieved grief, pulling you back forever, and
that every Returned has chosen *more life* and so seeded *more sorrow.* So you choose the second death the
Wall always promised you — freely, with love — and in doing so you give the loop the one thing it never
had: an **end.** The Unmade, finally allowed to grieve and be done, dissolves into peace. There will be no
next Returned. The Wall remains (the world is not saved), but the *machine of sorrow* beneath it stops.
You die. Truly. So the next forgotten soul might be *free* instead of *you.*
- **Final image:** the niche on the Wall with your name — and your true name spoken at last, willingly,
  as you step into it not to dissolve but to *rest,* and the chorus of the Unmade falls, one voice at a
  time, blessedly silent.
- **Tone:** Planescape's answer. *What can change the nature of a soul?* — choosing its own ending. The
  most quietly devastating ending; the one people will lose sleep over.

## 6. 🌾 MORTAL MEASURE — *"The Small Good"*
**Choice:** Refuse all of it — Crown, Wall, loop, and cause — and simply *live* your borrowed time.
**Requires:** decline every faction's apotheosis; low engagement with the cosmic thread; high personal
bonds.

You walk away. Let gods be gods and absences be absences. You spend your remaining days on the small,
human good in front of you — a companion's wedding, a child fed, a city's one good season. The cosmic
machinery grinds on without you; the Wall stands; the Unmade waits. But *you* were kind, and present, and
real, to the handful of people within arm's reach. The world is not fixed. You mattered anyway.
- **Final image:** a hearth, a small table, the people who survived the story, an ordinary evening — and
  outside the window, the long dark, patient, unremarked.
- **Tone:** the humblest, warmest ending; the refusal of grandeur as its own kind of grace.

---

## 🕷️ (Hidden failure-state) — *"The Husk"*
If the party is wiped at the Convergence, or you cede every choice to the Crown-Voice, Myrkul sheds Aldric,
takes the throne, and the Unmade — denied its champion — pulls back the *next* you to begin again. The
screen reads a single line: **"You came back. You always do."** New-game-plus seed; the loop, unbroken.

## Epilogue slides (per companion / faction — BG2 style)
Each ending plays a sequence of personalized cards keyed to flags. A sampler:

- **Sister Garrow** — *Keyhole:* becomes the first High Doomguide of the rewritten law, weeping with relief
  at her first honest judgment. *Doomguide's Peace:* keeps the old faith, and the doubt, until it kills her.
  *Break the Loop:* speaks your true name at every funeral she gives, so it is never again forgotten.
- **Roen** — *(romanced, survives):* leaves the Harpers; the card is just the two of you, an inn, no mission.
  *(Break the Loop):* the only person who knew your name, keeping it like a song. *(betrayed):* a Harper
  blade, a brief regret.
- **Varra** — *pact broken:* free, insufferable, alive, finally worth more than her price. *pact kept:* a
  bargain comes due in the dark.
- **Naeve** — *Keyhole:* burns Karsus's Last Theorem unused and teaches children arithmetic that builds, not
  ends. *Abolition:* hands the Unmade the theorem and watches a second sky fall, smiling, undone.
- **Ilfaeril** — *The Verdict forgiven:* lays down his sword after ten thousand years and simply *rests.*
  *martyred:* holds the Wall open so the damned can flee, and is the last one through — or isn't.
- **Maerin** — *saved:* a fading girl given one true year, godless and laughing. *rested:* returns to the
  Wall by choice to anchor the rewrite, freeing thousands; her last line breaks Aldric and the player both.
  *Abolition:* alive forever in the Deathless Garden, sixteen and smiling and *static,* which is the cruelest
  card in the game.
- **Aldric** — *redeemed:* tends graves on the Sword Coast, no longer a god, finally a father who buried his
  child. *ascended:* a husk wearing a crown, Myrkul's puppet, weeping behind the eyes.
- **Faction cards** — Kelemvor's church reforms or calcifies; the Choir is martyred or vindicated; the
  Harpers mourn or are purged; the Unbroken (Jergal's hidden faithful) inherit the rewritten afterlife or
  vanish unrealized.
