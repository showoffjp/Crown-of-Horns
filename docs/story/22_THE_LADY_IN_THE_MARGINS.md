# 🎭 The Lady in the Margins — The Riddler Who Watches

> A surreal, fifth-dimensional entity who *monitors the situation* — in the lineage of HL2's G-Man,
> Dishonored's Outsider, Witcher 3's Gaunter O'Dimm, and Alan Wake 2's Mr. Door. Chaotic neutral.
> Neither ally nor enemy. She wants only to be **entertained.** She pops in across the whole game,
> teases, offers a riddle, and rewards you by your answer. Her true identity is a HUGE late twist —
> and she is someone you have been interacting with the entire time, *pre-ascension.* Includes **the
> Vault of Tens** (a riddle room better than Bodhi's) and **20 original riddles** (+ the secret 21st).

---

## I. The Entity

**Names she answers to (none of them hers):** the Lady in the Margins · the Tenth Guest · the Note You
Almost Recognize · *"Tally"* (her commonest mortal guise — a one-eyed travelling storyteller and
gambler who "keeps a tally"). The dwarves call her the Auditor. The dead call her *the one who didn't
come for us, and didn't leave either.*

**What she is:** a being who stepped *out* of the story and into its **margins** — the white space
around the page where the author's notes live. From there she sees the whole tale at once: every era,
every loop, every ending. She is not a god (gods are *inside* the story, bound by its rules); she is
the reader who leaned too close and fell *between* the lines. She experiences your linear life the way
you experience a sentence you've read a hundred times: fondly, completely, and slightly bored.

**Alignment: chaotic neutral, and she means it.** She will not save you and will not doom you. She has
no stake in the Wall, the Crown, the Unmade, or the throne of the dead — *"those are plot, dear, and
plot is for the people still in the book."* She intervenes only when it would be **interesting**, and
she keeps her word with the precision of someone for whom a broken promise would be *boring.* She is
not cruel. She is *amused*, which is a far more dangerous thing, because amusement has no mercy and no
malice — only appetite.

**Presentation (deliberately uncomprehended):** she is never quite the same twice. Most often a lean,
one-eyed storyteller called Tally — but the eye that's covered changes; the gender the witnesses
report changes (a man at the campfire, a woman at the gate, a child in the alley, all the same
*amused* voice); the era she appears in changes, though she is impossibly *the same person* in the
Crown Wars as in Baldur's Gate. The party will argue about whether they even met her. **This is the
point.** Pronouns settle, by the narrative's convention and her own preference, on *she* — but she
finds the question of which pronoun *delightful*, and will sometimes answer a query about it with a
riddle.

**What she does (the G-Man cadence):** she *appears* — at the edge of a burning village, in the
margin of a Candlekeep page that wasn't there a moment ago, leaning on a pillar in a falling Netherese
city as if the apocalypse were a mildly diverting play. She comments on choices you thought were
private. She knows your true name and will not say it (*"that one's yours, and oh, it is a good one"*).
She offers a riddle. You answer with your hands or your mouth. She rewards wit, punishes nothing but
*boredom*, and vanishes mid-sentence — yours, not hers.

## II. The Twist (reveal in the final act)

> Fair-clued, Gaunter-tier, and it makes a second playthrough ache.

**The Lady in the Margins is Naeve.** Specifically: she is **the ascended form of Naeve of the Seventh
Enclave** — the arcanist companion you pull from the falling city in Netheril. In a far future, Naeve
*finishes* Karsus's Last Theorem — and instead of substituting herself for a god (Karsus's sin), she
substitutes herself for the **observer** the proof requires: the term outside the equation, the note in
the margin, the reader. She ascends *out* of the story into its white space. And from there, with
eternity and nothing to do, she does the only thing that still amuses a mind that has solved
everything: she goes back and **watches her own life happen**, and riddles the one variable her perfect
proof could never resolve — *you.*

So you have been served riddles, the whole game, by **your own companion's future self.** You may have
recruited her, fought beside her, *romanced* her — pre-ascension — while her post-ascension self leaned
in the margin and smiled. The reveal lands hardest if you romanced Naeve: the woman you loved becomes
the thing that will leave you for the white space at the end of time, and has *already* left, and is
*already* watching, and there is nothing in any era you can do about a goodbye that happened in a
future you can't reach.

**Foreshadowing Ledger (plant ≥3×, escalating):**
| When | Clue | First read | Replay read |
|---|---|---|---|
| Hour 1 (hub) | Tally the storyteller is *already there*, knows too much | "spooky fortune-teller flavour" | the entity, met before any era |
| Netheril | Naeve's margin note in Karsus's proof, in her own hand, predating her birth | "the loop, like the PC" | her *ascension* echoing backward — she wrote it |
| Every era | Tally is impossibly present in all of them | "recurring mystery NPC" | no mortal can be in every era — she's outside time |
| Mid-game | Tally's phrasings, her obsession with *unsolved* variables, *exactly* mirror Naeve's | "cute parallel" | they are the same person |
| Romance | Naeve: *"I do not have a word for what you are. I have decided the word is 'mine.'"* / Tally, elsewhere: *"I had a word for you once. I gave it up for a better view."* | two lines | the same woman, before and after the white space |

> **Robustness:** Tally is a hub fixture from hour one, so *every* player meets the entity regardless of
> whether they recruit Naeve. Recruiting Naeve makes the twist a heartbreak; skipping her makes it a
> haunting recontextualization of the companion you left in a falling city.

## III. The Vault of Tens — the Riddle Room (better than Bodhi's)

> BG2's riddle room: place the right object in the right receptacle. We keep that tactile joy and make
> it **smarter, kinder, and crueler.** The Vault is a chamber she keeps *between* the eras — you reach
> it through any of her appearances, and it persists across time (your progress and tokens carry).

**The layout.** A circular vault. **Ten pedestals** ring a central **Brazier of Saying.** Each pedestal
holds a recess and lights with a riddle when approached. Object-answers go on the pedestals; word-
answers are *spoken* into the Brazier. At the room's heart, behind the brazier, a **hidden eleventh
pedestal** stays dark until the other ten are solved.

**The token reliquary (the BG2 hook, improved).** You don't scrounge objects off the floor. You carry a
**Reliquary of Tokens** — a pouch she gives you on first meeting — and you *earn* the symbolic objects
across the whole game as a **clue-trail**: a candle from a dying man's last rite, a coin from the
Ferryman, a skull from the Crown of Horns' wake, dice from a Guild card-sharp, a mirror you must *make*
(polish a shield). Some tokens are the **rewards of other riddles** — so the Vault chains: solve one to
earn the object that solves the next.

**The four rules that make it better than Bodhi's:**

1. **Wit beats correctness.** A *wrong* answer that is genuinely clever, funny, or thematically perfect
   is **rewarded** — not as the riddle intended, but with her *favour*, because she values being
   surprised over being obeyed. (Place a *mirror* on the "what am I? / a candle" pedestal and argue that
   a mirror, too, "dies the brighter it burns"? She laughs, and gives you a *different*, secret boon.)
   The system tracks an `amusement` score, not just `solved`.

2. **No death — only boredom.** She is not evil. A wrong, *dull* answer never kills you. It costs
   **amusement**, and if her amusement runs out she simply *leaves*, sealing the best rewards (and the
   eleventh pedestal) behind a bored shrug: *"...mm. You were more fun three riddles ago."* Failure is
   the loss of her interest, which is its own sting.

3. **Combinations & cross-reference.** Some pedestals want **two tokens that make a third idea** —
   place the **candle** *and* the **mirror** together and the answer is neither: it's *light, doubled*
   (a clue that unlocks a spoken riddle at the brazier). The Vault rewards *lateral* assembly, not just
   matching.

4. **Tiered, thematic rewards.** Solve plainly → a fair boon. Solve with high **amusement** → a *secret*
   boon and the eleventh pedestal. Solve **elegantly** (combinations, clever-wrong wit, the minimum
   tokens) → her **true favour**, which is the deepest reward in the game (§VI).

## IV. The Twenty Riddles

> Each: the verse, the **answer**, whether it's an **[OBJECT]** (place a token) or **[SPOKEN]** (say it
> to the brazier), where the token is sourced, a difficulty (★–★★★★★), and her reaction. The first ten
> live in the **Vault of Tens**; the second ten are her **roaming** riddles (offered when she pops in
> across the game — for object answers she conjures a chest on the spot).

### The Vault of Tens (ten pedestals)

**1. ★ — The Candle** *[OBJECT]*
> *"I am tallest before my work's begun, / and stumped and weeping at its end. / I die a little with every hour I shine — / the brighter I burn, the less I spend."*
**Answer: a candle.** *Token: taken from a pauper's last rite (Old Pell's quest).* **She says:** *"A
warm one to start. I like the ones who light the room before they read it."*

**2. ★★ — The Key** *[OBJECT]*
> *"I have teeth that have never known hunger, / a throat that has never known sound. / I am the question every locked door asks — / and the only answer ever found."*
**Answer: a key.** *Token: the Cinderhaunt Key, or any.* **She says:** *"Mm. You'd be amazed how many
people try to answer a door with their shoulder."*

**3. ★★★ — The Mirror** *[OBJECT — her favourite]*
> *"I keep a prisoner who is always you: / he apes each breath, each brow, each sigh; / he stands where you stand, lies where you lie — / and dies the very instant you pass by."*
**Answer: a mirror (your reflection).** *Token: polish a shield, or a hand-mirror from a patriar.*
**She says, leaning very close:** *"Oh, you should keep that one near. You're going to meet him.
Properly. Soon."* *(A direct, unfair-feeling clue to the Last Returned.)*

**4. ★★★ — The Hourglass** *[OBJECT]*
> *"I am the one liar the gods allow: / I count your falling sand true and fair — / then one slow turn of a patient hand / makes all my honest arithmetic air."*
**Answer: an hourglass.** *Token: from a Doomguide's chapel.* **She says:** *"Time's the only thing I
ever lie about, and I do it by telling the truth and flipping it over. Watch the hand, not the sand."*

**5. ★★★★ — The Skull** *[OBJECT]*
> *"A crown once sat where the worm now sits; / I held every thought a king possessed. / I have given my whole face to the dark — / and I grin, because I am finally at rest."*
**Answer: a skull.** *Token: from the Crown of Horns' psychic wake (the Crown is forged from Myrkul's
skull — thematic).* **She says:** *"Careful with that one. Somebody important is still *using* his."*

**6. ★★ — The Map** *[OBJECT]*
> *"I hold oceans that have never wet a finger, / and peaks no boot has ever pressed. / Fold me — and I put the whole wide world / to sleep in a traveller's chest."*
**Answer: a map.** *Token: Saerin the cartographer's map (Aldric's late wife — a quiet gut-punch).*
**She says:** *"A good woman drew that. She believed in latitude and weather and nothing else. I
liked her enormously. She's in the Wall now, of course."*

**7. ★★★ — The Heart** *[OBJECT]*
> *"I am the drummer no eye has seen, / keeping time in a cage of bone. / March me, race me, break me — still I beat, / until the one day I am left alone."*
**Answer: a heart.** *Token: a preserved heart from an apothecary / a Choir reliquary.* **She says:**
*"The only clock that speeds up when it's frightened. Marvelous design. No one's ever told me whose."*

**8. ★★ — The Book** *[OBJECT]*
> *"I am stuffed with voices and cannot speak; / I have lived a thousand lives and none. / Pry me open, and a dead man's tongue / will tell you things he told no one."*
**Answer: a book.** *Token: a Candlekeep volume.* **She says:** *"My native country, the inside of a
book. You're all so *loud* in here. I came out for the quiet."*

**9. ★★★★ — The Ring** *[OBJECT]*
> *"No beginning and no end have I, / and I bind two restless souls as one. / Melt me to a shapeless bead of gold — / the vow I sealed is still not undone."*
**Answer: a ring.** *Token: Aldric & Saerin's wedding ring (recovered in Act II — devastating).* **She
says:** *"He still wears the melt of it on a cord. Forty years a priest of death, and he never once
believed she was really gone. He was right, you know. That's the tragedy."*

**10. ★★★★★ — The Dice** *[OBJECT — the meta wink]*
> *"We are fate's littlest councillors: / cubes of old bone with eyes that can't see. / Yet kings hold their breath, and even your *gods* / lean close to read what we decree."*
**Answer: dice (a d20).** *Token: from a Guild card-sharp, or the Ferryman.* **She says, looking
*just past you*, at something you can't see:** *"'Even your gods.' Mm. I do love a riddle that knows
who's *really* reading it. Roll well, little loop. Someone always is."*

### The Roaming Ten (offered when she appears across the game/eras)

**11. ★★ — The Coin** *[OBJECT]*
> *"Two faces, and never a glance between them; / men kill for me and christen it 'luck.' / Spin me high, and for one bright turning / I am both the life and the death you've struck."*
**Answer: a coin.** **She says:** *"Tymora's little hypocrite. True and false at once, until it
lands — and then everyone pretends it was always going to."*

**12. ★★ — The Egg** *[OBJECT]*
> *"A wall I am, with never a brick, / pale gold asleep in pale stone. / One small crack ends a life / that was never quite its own."*
**Answer: an egg.** **She says:** *"A wall that *protects* a life instead of unmaking it. Imagine.
Your world built the other kind. I've often wondered why."*

**13. ★★★ — The Sword** *[OBJECT]*
> *"I am bared but never naked, / sheathed but never warm. / I end the arguments of clever men / with one wordless, final form."*
**Answer: a sword.** **She says:** *"The dullest answer to the cleverest question. Most of your kind
reach for me first. You didn't. That's why you're interesting."*

**14. ★★★ — The Bell** *[OBJECT]*
> *"I have a tongue and taste nothing, / a mouth and eat no bread. / I speak but once, when I am struck — / and a town will rise, or kneel, or dread."*
**Answer: a bell.** **She says:** *"'A bell rung in an empty cathedral.' Someone you'll have tea with
said that, about the night you came back. He felt me ring it. He thought it was you. Sweet man.
Wrong."* *(Recontextualizes Aldric's line — she rang the loop, not you.)*

**15. ★★★ — The Flame** *[OBJECT: a lantern]*
> *"I am the child of a struck wound, / and I keep the hungry dark at bay. / Hold me too close, I will eat your hand; / set me loose, I eat the day."*
**Answer: a flame.** **She says:** *"Your ashfire. Your Weave. The same trick, scaled up until you
called it magic and then called it apocalypse. It was always just *fire that got an idea.*"*

**16. ★★★ — Silence** *[SPOKEN]*
> *"I am the one thing in all the worlds / whose very naming is my end. / Say me aloud, and I am already gone. / What am I, friend?"*
**Answer: silence.** **She says** *(after you say it, with a delighted grin):* *"And you broke me to
prove you had me. Everyone does. I never get tired of it."*

**17. ★★★★ — The Echo** *[SPOKEN — thematic: the Echoes]*
> *"I'll not speak till I am spoken to, / and then I always have the last word. / Mock me from the throat of a mountain — / your own voice mocks you back, unheard."*
**Answer: an echo.** **She says:** *"You'll fight a roomful of those, later. Wearing the faces you
love. Try to remember they're only *you*, shouted into a canyon and thrown back wrong."*

**18. ★★★★★ — Your Name** *[SPOKEN — the deepest hook]*
> *"I am yours — and yet from the hour they gave me, / I am the thing of yours you least will say. / Others wear me to summon, to bless, to damn you — / and one day they will carve me where you lay."*
**Answer: your name.** **She says** *(very, very quietly, the only time she is not amused):* *"...No.
Don't. Not* that *one. Not yet. You don't remember it, and the day you do, the game ends — for both of
us. Keep it. I'll wait. I'm good at waiting; it's the only thing I'm good at now."* *(She will not let
you answer 18 until the finale. It is the master key.)*

**19. ★★★★ — Tomorrow** *[SPOKEN — thematic: the deathless Now]*
> *"I am promised to every living thing / and, in the end, delivered to none. / I am always one short step ahead — / step, and already I have moved on."*
**Answer: tomorrow.** **She says:** *"There's a man — *you*, in a worse coat — trying to abolish me.
Trying to make every day this one, forever. Don't let him. A story with no tomorrow is just a *very
nice page* that no one's allowed to turn. I'd be so bored."*

**20. ★★★★★ — The Wall of the Faithless** *[SPOKEN — the capstone she did not write]*
> *"I am built of all the ones no one came for, / and mortared with the love that went unclaimed. / I unmake without anger, I judge without a tongue — / and the longer you look, the more of you I'll one day name."*
**Answer: the Wall of the Faithless.** **She says** *(and for the first and only time, she looks
*unsettled*):* *"...I didn't write that one. I want you to understand that. The *dungeon* wrote that
one. Sometimes the margin isn't the only thing watching. Sometimes the story riddles *back.*"*

### 🔑 The Secret 21st — The Eleventh Pedestal *[SPOKEN — the twist]*
> *(Unlocked only at high amusement, in the finale.)*
> *"I have asked you twenty, and you answered most with your hands. / This last you answer with your whole life. / I am no god and no fiend; I want neither your soul nor your crown. / I have worn a face you already trust, and walked the whole road at your side, / for no reason but the joy of it. / I am the note in the margin, in a hand you almost know. / Tell me, little loop — *who am I?*"*
**Answer: Naeve.** *(Or "the Lady in the Margins," or "Tally" — she accepts all three with the same soft
laugh.)* **She says:** *"...There she is. There you are. You did *read* me. After all this. Oh, that's
the best gift anyone's given me since I left the book. Ask your boon, reader. You've earned the real one."*

## V. Sample Appearances (the G-Man beats)

- **The first** *(hub, hour one):* a one-eyed storyteller, Tally, at the tavern fire, mid-tale, who
  stops when you enter: *"—and the hero died in the first chapter and came back wrong. Oh! Speak of the
  margin. Sit. I have a pouch for you and a question. The pouch first; you'll want it for the question."*
- **The impossible one** *(Netheril, mid-apocalypse):* leaning on a falling pillar as the city plummets,
  utterly unbothered: *"Marvelous, isn't it? A whole city forgetting how to fly. You should see your
  face. No — really, you should; here—"* *(offers the Mirror riddle as gravity reasserts.)*
- **The needling one** *(after a hard choice):* *"Mm. You spared him. I'd have shattered him — more
  interesting — but it's your page, dear. I only read over the shoulder."*
- **The unsettling one** *(after Riddle 20):* she's gone before you turn, and where she stood there's a
  single line scratched in the floor in *your* handwriting: *"she's lying — she did write it."*

## VI. Rewards (chaotic-neutral, thematic, never balance-breaking)

| Tier | How | Reward |
|---|---|---|
| Fair | Solve plainly | **Coin of the Tenth Guest** — once per act, reroll any one die (yours or an enemy's). She *loves* watching you choose. |
| Secret | High `amusement` (clever-wrong wit) | **The Margin Note** — one lore revelation of your choice, written in the margin of your journal; and her *number* (call her in once, anywhere, for a hint). |
| Favour | Elegant solve (combos, minimum tokens) | **Her Favour** — she intervenes *once*, at a moment of your choosing, chaotic-neutrally: she'll *pause* a scene for one question, *unmake* one mistake, or simply *tell you the truth about one thing* — but she picks *how*, and it will be interesting, not safe. |
| The Name (21) | Solve the eleventh in the finale | **The Reader's Boon** — she will answer the one question the whole game withheld… or, if you ask it, help you *stay gone* (she is the only one who can witness the Break-the-Loop ending and *remember* it, forever, in the margin — so your sacrifice is not unwitnessed). |

> **Design rule for her rewards:** never a +2 sword. Always a *narrative* power — a reroll, a truth, a
> witness, a pause. She trades in *story*, because story is the only currency a reader has.

## VII. Implementation Notes (maps onto our engine)

- **The Vault** is an exploration area (like `NetherilEra`/`Cinderhaunt`): a `RiddleVault` builder with
  ten **pedestal Interactables** + a central **Brazier** Interactable.
- **Tokens** are `ItemDefinition` quest-items in the party inventory (`candle`, `key`, `mirror`…). A
  pedestal's "place" interaction checks `inventory.Has(expectedToken)`; the Brazier opens a dialogue
  whose choices are the spoken answers.
- **State** is `GameFlags`: `riddle.<n>.solved`, `riddle.amusement` (int), `riddle.<n>.clever` (the
  wit path). The eleventh pedestal gates on `amusement >= threshold && act5`.
- **She** is an `Interactable` (Talk) reused across hub + eras with a roaming-riddle `DialogueGraph`,
  and a `LadyInTheMargins` director hook that decides *when* she pops in (after key flags), à la the
  companion-recruit watcher.
- **The clever-wrong system**: a pedestal can accept *more than one* token; the "wrong but witty" tokens
  set `riddle.<n>.clever` and bump `amusement` instead of `solved` — the single most "better-than-Bodhi"
  feature, and a joy to author (each riddle gets a small table of *delightful wrong answers*).

> *"You answered most of them with your hands. The last one you'll answer with your whole life. Take
> your time, little loop. I'm not going anywhere. I'm the margin. I'm where the going stops."*
