# 🌄 The Endings — Full Prose

> Each of the six endings (plus the hidden failure-state) written as a complete epilogue sequence —
> the final scene, then the "where they are now" slides, then the last image. The mechanics and flag
> requirements live in `04_ENDINGS.md`; this is the *experience.* Every ending lands the cosmos on a
> single face (the `anchor`, per `13` §V).

> Read these as the credits-roll narration would play them: slow, spare, earned.

---

## 1. 🜍 ABOLITION-BY-ATROCITY — *"The Mercy That Ate the World"*

You say the name, and you say *yes*, and you pull the thread.

It is the most beautiful thing anyone has ever done, for about an hour. Across every age at once the
Wall unravels — niche by niche, soul by soul, ten thousand years of the discarded coming loose all
together like a held breath finally, finally let go. Saerin, freed. The Crown-War damned, freed. The
Netherese dead, the boy in the window, the small forgotten everyones — freed. *Maerin*, freed, whole,
forever. You did it. You actually did it.

And then the bill, itemized exactly as Karsus's theorem promised, comes due. Death itself was the
mortar holding the world's *sequence* together — before and after, cause and effect, the quiet
machinery that lets one moment become the next. Unbind it, and the living do not become immortal. They
become *simultaneous.* Time knots shut into a single deathless Now, and into that Now you climb, the
Unmade's avatar, the steward of every saved soul, and you take your seat at the center of the most
beautiful tomb in the history of creation.

*Epilogue — the anchor:* The final image is the one you love, in the Deathless Garden, mid-laugh, lit
gold. *(If Maerin is the anchor:)* sixteen, alive, smiling — the same smile, the same instant, forever,
one syllable short of the end of her sentence, while you sit across the garden and are permitted, for
all eternity, to never have to grieve her. She never finishes. None of them ever finish. You saved them
all from ever ending, which means you saved them from ever *continuing*, and you will have ten thousand
times ten thousand years to understand the difference, and you will spend every one of them telling
yourself it was worth it, and you will be the only one left who can still wonder, because wondering is
change, and you are the last changing thing in a saved and frozen world.

*Last line (the Last Returned, gone now, its voice the last thing that fades):* "...You did exactly what
I did. I'm so sorry. I'm so sorry. Welcome home. There's no one here. There's everyone here. There's
no difference anymore. Welcome home."

## 2. ⚖️ THE DOOMGUIDE'S PEACE — *"The Atrocity Kept"*

You side with Vayle. You stop Aldric. You beat the Unmade back into the dark — not *answered*, not
*grieved*, just *suppressed*, the way you suppress a scream you've decided you cannot afford. The Crown
of Horns goes back into the deepest vault. The Wall stands. Death's order endures.

And you live with it.

*Epilogue — the church:* Kelemvor's Doomguides rebuild; the harvest-sites are cleansed; the Unbound are
laid. Order returns to the Sword Coast, grey and disciplined and *safe.* Children sleep behind walls
that hold.

*Epilogue — Vayle:* She dies within the year, of the illness she hid, and she dies Faithless, and she
goes — by her own iron consent — into the Wall she spent her life guarding. Her last words to you, if
you're there: *"Don't look so stricken. I always knew the cell I was building. I just decided a known
hell was better than an unknown apocalypse. Tell me I was wrong. You can't, can you. That's the worst
part. Neither could I."*

*Epilogue — the anchor:* You and the one you love stand at the potter's field as the unclaimed dead go
into the pauper graves. You saved the world. You also looked at the oldest cruelty in the multiverse,
understood it *completely*, and defended it — because the alternatives were worse, and you were not
wrong, and being not-wrong is the heaviest thing you have ever carried.

*Last image:* the Wall, intact, a single new niche being filled — and you, turning away from it,
because you cannot bear to watch and cannot bring yourself to stop it, which is, you understand now,
exactly the posture of every god who ever let it stand.

## 3. 🗝️ JERGAL'S KEYHOLE — *"The Tedious Mercy"* (the golden ending)

You do the hardest thing, which is also the smallest: you *neither* tear the Wall down *nor* keep it.
You *rewrite* it.

Jergal, delighted past his ten thousand years of boredom, hands you the pen he laid down at the dawn of
death. Naeve derives the new Law; Ilfaeril witnesses it; the Mournlight — your own grief, on your side
at last — holds the door. And the Unmade, *seen* for the first time, its dead acknowledged, its
arithmetic finally *corrected* instead of obeyed, chooses — for the first time in ten thousand years —
peace over unmaking. The Wall comes down not all at once, in a cataclysm, but *slowly*, soul by soul,
each one **judged** — by a hand, honestly, by name — instead of dissolved.

It will take you the rest of eternity. You take the job.

*Epilogue — the Ledger:* You sit at a desk at the end of the world, the great Ledger of the Unwalled
open before you, and you call the first discarded soul forward by its true name — and it is *yours*, the
First Discarded, ten thousand years old — and you weigh it, honestly, and you forgive it, and you let it
rest. And then the next. And then the next. Forever.

*Epilogue — the companions:* Garrow becomes the first High Doomguide of the rewritten Law, weeping at
her first honest judgment. Ilfaeril, his ten-thousand-year vote finally *un-cast*, lays down his sword
and is, at last, allowed to stop paying. Naeve, if romanced, sits at the desk beside you — *"An infinite
proof, one soul at a time. I can imagine no better forever."*

*Last image:* heaven exhaling. The first full breath the afterlife has taken in ten thousand years. And
you, at the desk, calling the next name, and the next, and the next — the triumph that costs you
eternity and is, without question, worth it.

## 4. 👑 THE RETURNED THRONE — *"The New Tyrant, Maybe Merciful"*

You take the Crown of Horns yourself. You become the god of the dead.

And you become, precisely, the thing you spent the whole game watching others become — which the game
neither condemns nor spares you. The epilogue forks hard on who you *were* along the way:

*If merciful:* You reform the afterlife from the throne — a lesser, lonelier Keyhole, the Wall eased but
not unmade, the dead judged by a *god's* hand instead of a clerk's, which is faster and crueler and
still so much better than what was. You are a good god, as gods go. You are also alone in a way no mortal
can imagine, and you will be for as long as the throne lasts, which is forever, which is the point.

*If you climbed on cruelty:* You are the seed of the next catastrophe. Somewhere, someday, a future
Returned will have to walk back through history to stop the thing *you* became, and they will find your
face under the hood, aged and certain, and you will tell them it was for the best, and you will believe
it, and the wheel will turn.

*Last image:* you, crowned, on Kelemvor's vacated throne, the dead lined up before you to the horizon —
and a faint, familiar warmth as the *next* crack begins, somewhere, in some other now, because a mortal
took the chair again, because they always do, because the chair was never the answer and you knew it and
you sat down anyway.

## 5. 🕯️ BREAK THE LOOP — *"Stay Gone"* (the existential ending)

You lower your weapon. You say your true name. And you do the one thing, in ten thousand years, that the
loop has never seen you do.

You choose the ending.

You understand, finally and entirely, that the Unmade is *you* — your own grief, ungrieved, pulling
yourself back across all of time, because every Returned chose *more*: more life, more saving, more
mercy, and every "more" seeded the sorrow that pulled the next one back. The only way out was never
strength, or cleverness, or even kindness. It was the courage to want, at last, *less.* To let one
ending — just one — actually happen.

So you walk to the niche on the Wall that has had your name on it since before the world was young, and
you step into it — not to dissolve, this time, but to *rest* — and the Unmade, hearing its own first
name spoken willingly, with love, by the only soul who could, finds the one thing it never had in ten
thousand years of grieving: an *outside.* A witness. A place to set the grief down.

The chorus falls silent, one voice at a time. There will be no next Returned. The Wall remains — the
world is not saved, the cruelty endures — but the *engine of sorrow* beneath it, the machine that was
you, finally, blessedly, *stops.*

*Epilogue — the anchor:* The last thing you feel is a hand in yours. *(Garrow gives the rite, speaking
your name so it is never forgotten; or Roen holds your hand with no joke left, just "I found you once,
in all of it — that's enough, go on, I've got you"; or Maerin, saved, watches the one who freed her
choose to go, and whispers "I'll be worth it — I swear I'll be worth it.")* The galaxy ends on that
hand, that face, that single human grief — *moving*, the way the Deathless Garden's never could.

*Last image:* the empty niche, no longer empty, no longer dissolving. A soul at rest. And, very far
away, the next forgotten person who dies godless — and is *not* pulled back — and is allowed, for the
first time in ten thousand years, to simply, freely, *be gone.* You did that. For them. For a stranger
you will never meet, in a world you will never see. The most selfless thing anyone has ever done, and no
one will ever know, and that is exactly why it counts.

## 6. 🌾 MORTAL MEASURE — *"The Small Good"*

You refuse all of it. The Crown, the Wall, the loop, the cause. You walk away from the end of the world
and you go *home.*

The cosmic machinery grinds on without you. The Wall stands; the Unmade waits; somewhere, someday, it
will pull someone back, and maybe it will be you again and maybe it won't, and you decide — with the
full weight of everything you now know — that this is not yours to solve today. Today there is bread to
buy. Today there is a friend's wedding. Today there is one good season in one small city, and you are
*in* it, present and kind and real.

*Epilogue:* You spend your borrowed time on the people within arm's reach. Old Pell's wall of the named
godless grows by your hand. The widower's garden gets tended. Nib and Tully grow up with someone who
showed up. The world is not fixed. The great wound goes unhealed. And a dozen small human lives are
warmer because you were in them, and chose them, over the apocalypse.

*Last image:* a hearth, a small table, the people who survived the story, an ordinary evening — and
outside the window, the long dark, patient and unremarked, and you, for once, not looking at it. Just
passing the bread. Just being *here.* The humblest grace in the game, and not the smallest.

## 🕷️ (Hidden) — *"The Husk"*

If the party falls at the Convergence, or you cede every choice to the Crown-Voice, Myrkul sheds Aldric
like a coat and takes the throne, and the Unmade — denied its champion — does the only thing it knows.
It reaches into the Wall. It pulls one back.

The screen holds black. A single line:

**"You came back. You always do."**

*(New Game+ seeds: the niche half-formed, the note already written. The loop, unbroken, waiting for the
hand that will, someday, in some cycle, finally let it end.)*

---

> **The throughline of all six:** the game refuses to tell you which ending is *right.* The most
> beautiful one is the most damning; the kindest one costs you your existence; the humblest one saves
> no one and matters anyway. Each is a complete, coherent answer to the question the whole game asked —
> *what is a soul worth that no god ever claimed?* — and the answer the game believes is the one *you*
> are willing to live, or die, by.
